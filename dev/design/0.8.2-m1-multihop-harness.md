# M1 — multi-hop answer-accuracy harness: design + pre-registration

> **Purpose.** Freeze, *before any data is seen*, the instrument that fairly adjudicates whether a
> knowledge graph improves multi-hop **answer accuracy** (EM/F1) on MuSiQue. This is step 1 of 3
> (M1/M2/S1). The primary endpoint, hop strata, decision rule, and power plan below are **frozen and
> dated**; the GO/NO-GO computation is additionally frozen as code in
> `src/python/eval/m1_decision_rule.py::decide()` so a downstream slice (Slice 20) cannot post-hoc
> switch the endpoint. `decide()` is imported, never redefined.

## 0. Why this instrument (and why it is fair)

The 0.8.1 "beat BM25" investigation reached a **robust but narrow NEGATIVE**
(`dev/plans/runs/0.8.1-beat-bm25-report.md`; [[graph-arm-doesnt-beat-bm25-pivot]]): it measured a
**raw-BFS** graph arm on **first-stage needle-recall** (the graph-disfavored axis) with
**self-seeding** (the graph's own FTS, not the lexical top-K). It says nothing about multi-hop
**answer accuracy**, the axis where graphs are *claimed* to help, because we never measured it and
lacked the instrument. M1 builds the instrument and tests the *strongest fair* lightweight graph
method, so the verdict — expected near-tie to modest loss (GraphRAG-Bench arXiv:2506.05690;
RAG-vs-GraphRAG arXiv:2502.11371) — converts *"we tried a weak BFS arm"* into *"we tried the
literature's best lightweight graph method and it still didn't win,"* the result that earns the
index-key-enrichment pivot.

Two corrections from the 0.8.1 post-mortem are baked into the design:
- **Not raw BFS.** Uniform BFS is the documented anti-pattern (subgraph blow-up + hub drift = our
  entity-co-mingling failure). The principled lightweight form is **HippoRAG-style lexically-seeded
  Personalized PageRank fused with BM25** (HippoRAG NeurIPS 2024 arXiv:2405.14831; HippoRAG 2
  arXiv:2502.14802). PPR strictly dominates BFS → if PPR-fusion can't beat the baseline, BFS never
  would. Clean adjudication.
- **Strong baseline, not a lexical strawman.** Graph wins vanish against a strong dense baseline, so
  the baseline arm is **BM25 ∪ passage-level dense ∪ fused(RRF)** — see §3.

## 1. Datasets

- **MuSiQue-Ans, distractor setting (~20 paragraphs/question)** — the arbiter (MuSiQue, TACL 2022,
  arXiv:2108.00573). Engineered to defeat shortcuts (3× the human–machine gap; ~30-pt F1 drop for
  single-hop/disconnected models vs prior sets). Two built-in properties make a measured lift
  *attributable to the index*, not to artifacts (the HotpotQA trap):
  - **2/3/4-hop split = a built-in hop-trend axis.** The **primary signal is the pooled ≥3-hop ΔF1**
    (§4); the per-hop splits are a pre-registered **secondary** trend read. The literature does **not**
    predict monotonic growth (HippoRAG: 4-hop path-finding is out of reach of single-pass PPR → expect
    flat or 3-hop-peaked), so the rule vetoes only on a **significantly negative** hop trend, never on
    the absence of growth.
  - **Unanswerable contrast set** — a confident-wrong-answer guard (graph must not induce confident
    wrong answers on the unanswerable set). Secondary, not primary.
- **License/provenance.** CC-BY-4.0 (`StonyBrookNLP/musique`). Acquired by a Slice-5 script,
  **gitignored**, pinned by a `musique_hash`. (Acquire script is **Slice 5**, not this slice.)
- **Deliberately NOT here:** 2WikiMultiHopQA, MultiHop-RAG → **0.8.3 (M2)**.

## 2. Arms

| Arm | What it is | Stage |
|---|---|---|
| **bm25** | FTS5/BM25 lexical retrieval (existing) | baseline component |
| **passage-dense** | passage-level embedder (existing; **not** whole-doc R4) | baseline component |
| **fused** | RRF(bm25, passage-dense), **RRF k=60 pinned** | baseline component |
| **fused+rerank** | `fused` re-ordered by the **0.8.1 R1 CPU cross-encoder** (`rerank_fused`) — the **strongest** baseline | **fixed comparator** (amendment 6) |
| **ppr-fusion** | lexically-seeded Personalized PageRank over the per-question graph, RRF-fused with BM25 | the graph arm under test |

> **The fixed comparator is `fused+rerank`** (amendments 2 + 6): every Δ in the primary endpoint is
> `(ppr-fusion) − (fused+rerank)`, a **single pre-registered arm**, never a per-hop max-of-arms.
> This removes the winner's-curse baseline inflation and the dose-response confound, and closes the
> weak-baseline false-GO loophole (a GO must not be attributable to "we didn't rerank"). The
> `fused+rerank` arm reuses the 0.8.1 R1 cross-encoder `rerank_fused` (built in Slice 5; only
> **specified** here). RRF fusion is pinned at **k=60** across every fused arm.

`ppr-fusion` mechanism (built in Slice 15, specified here): **(1) Seed** from BM25/FTS5 top-K →
map to graph entity nodes; **weight seeds by IDF / node specificity** (FTS5 term stats, free) to
suppress hub drift. **(2) Propagate** one Personalized-PageRank pass biased to those seeds;
deterministic (fixed teleport probability, fixed iteration cap + tolerance, stable node ordering).
**(3) Rank** passages by summed PPR mass; **fuse with BM25 via RRF** (HippoRAG-2's lesson:
graph+lexical fusion beats graph-alone and avoids entity-only context loss).

## 3. The strong-baseline definition (harness requirement, not optional)

> **The baseline arm set is `{BM25, passage-level dense, fused(RRF k=60), fused+rerank}` — NOT
> lexical-only. The primary-endpoint comparator is the single fixed `fused+rerank` arm.**

Measuring the graph arm against a lexical-only strawman is the methodological error that would
invalidate the verdict. Three constraints:
- **Passage-level dense, not whole-doc (R4).** Whole-doc vectors blur discrimination (our own
  measured failure mode; R4 stays parked in 0.8.5).
- **Fixed comparator, never per-hop max (amendment 2).** The primary endpoint compares `ppr-fusion`
  against the **single, pre-registered `fused+rerank` arm** — the strongest baseline. A per-hop
  max-of-arms is forbidden: it inflates the bar by the winner's curse (max of noisy estimates) *and*
  confounds the dose-response (a different arm can win each hop). The other three arms
  ({bm25, passage-dense, fused}) are reported for context but are **not** the comparator.
- **`fused+rerank` closes the weak-baseline loophole (amendment 6).** Reuse the 0.8.1 R1 CPU
  cross-encoder (`rerank_fused`); RRF k=60 pinned. Without it a GO could be attributable to "we
  didn't rerank" rather than to graph topology.

## 4. Pre-registration (FROZEN 2026-06-16; AMENDED 2026-06-16)

> Machine-checked by the schema-lint (`m1_decision_rule.lint_preregistration`) + the
> `test_m1_decision_rule.py` pre-registration test. Each `frozen-field:` line below is required and
> must carry a date; removing or undating any line fails the build.
>
> **AMENDED 2026-06-16 (HITL — all 6 pre-freeze methodology-review amendments adopted;
> `runs/0.8.2-slice-0-prereg-methodology-review.md`).** The original strict-monotonic
> `f1[2]<f1[3]<f1[4]` dose-response gate + per-hop-max baseline biased the rule toward the expected
> NO_GO. They are replaced below by (1) a **trend gate** (veto only on a significantly *negative*
> slope), (2) a **single fixed comparator** (`fused+rerank`), (3) a **pooled ≥3-hop ΔF1 material gate
> with a paired-bootstrap CI lower bound > 0**, (4) a **whole-`decide()`-rule power simulation**, (5)
> a **CI-banded (softened) EM gate** with the unanswerable-set carrying the confident-wrong role, and
> (6) the added **`fused+rerank` baseline arm** (RRF k=60 pinned). Every amendment moves the rule
> *less* conservative — the correct direction to scrutinize given the honest NO_GO prior.

- **frozen-field: primary-endpoint** (frozen 2026-06-16): the **paired pooled ≥3-hop (hops 3+4) ΔF1
  = (ppr-fusion) − (fused+rerank)** on the MuSiQue-Ans **answerable** set, via a **question-level
  paired bootstrap** (point estimate + BCa CI). **F1 is the primary continuous signal**
  (single-digit-F1 regime per the literature); pooled ≥3-hop ΔEM is a CI-banded secondary guard. The
  **per-hop (2/3/4) ΔF1/ΔEM splits are pre-registered SECONDARY** outcomes — they feed the trend read
  (below), not the primary GO test. The unanswerable-set confident-answer rate is a confident-wrong
  guard, **not** a primary endpoint.
- **frozen-field: comparator** (frozen 2026-06-16): the primary endpoint's comparator is the
  **single, fixed `fused+rerank` arm** — the strongest baseline (`fused` re-ordered by the 0.8.1 R1
  CPU cross-encoder `rerank_fused`). It is **never** a per-hop max-of-arms (that inflates the bar via
  the winner's curse and confounds the dose-response). All Δ = `(ppr-fusion) − (fused+rerank)`.
- **frozen-field: baseline-arms** (frozen 2026-06-16): the baseline arm set is
  **{bm25, passage-dense, fused (RRF k=60 pinned), fused+rerank}**; `fused+rerank` is the fixed
  comparator, the other three are reported for context. RRF fusion uses **k=60** in every fused arm.
- **frozen-field: decision-rule** (frozen 2026-06-16): the GO/NO-GO call is computed **mechanically**
  by `src/python/eval/m1_decision_rule.py::decide(material, em, trend, confident_wrong, power_ok)`;
  Slice 20 imports it and **may not redefine it**. `decide()` consumes **already-computed summary
  statistics** (the Slice-20 harness runs the paired bootstrap; `decide()` is pure/deterministic — no
  RNG, no I/O), where `material = {"f1_delta", "f1_ci_low"}`, `em = {"ci_high"}`,
  `trend = {"neg_significant"}`, `confident_wrong = {"increase_significant"}`. Prose form: **GO** iff
  **all** of —
  1. **material** — `material.f1_delta ≥ MATERIAL_F1_LIFT (0.02)` **and** `material.f1_ci_low > 0`
     (the pooled ≥3-hop ΔF1 is material *and* its paired-bootstrap CI excludes 0);
  2. **trend not significantly negative** — `not trend.neg_significant` (**veto only on a
     significantly NEGATIVE** ΔF1-vs-hop slope; flat or positive passes — **no strict-monotonic
     requirement**);
  3. **EM not significantly worse** — `em.ci_high ≥ 0` (CI-banded pooled ≥3-hop ΔEM check, **not** a
     point-estimate veto);
  4. **no confident-wrong increase** — `not confident_wrong.increase_significant` (the
     **unanswerable-set** confident-answer rate carries this role, not EM);
  5. **adequately powered** — `power_ok` is True.
  Otherwise **NO_GO**. Frozen threshold: `MATERIAL_F1_LIFT = 0.02` (2 F1 points). **Non-finite
  (`NaN`/±inf) floats in `material`/`em` raise `ValueError`** (kept from the Slice-0 fix-1 guard — a
  malformed endpoint must fail loudly, never silently return a verdict).
- **frozen-field: trend-test** (frozen 2026-06-16): the trend signal is the **sign of the OLS slope
  of per-question ΔF1 regressed on hop count** (hops 2/3/4); `trend.neg_significant` is True iff that
  slope is **significantly negative** under the question-level paired bootstrap (its BCa CI for the
  slope lies entirely below 0). A flat (≈0) or positive slope is **not** a veto. This replaces the
  old strict-`<` monotonic gate, which the literature contradicts (HippoRAG: 4-hop path-finding is
  out of reach of single-pass PPR → expect flat or 3-hop-peaked, not monotonic growth) and which
  passes only ~1/6 of the time even under a true uniform positive Δ.
- **frozen-field: mde-power-plan** (frozen 2026-06-16): power is sized by a **whole-`decide()`-rule
  power simulation**, **not** a marginal per-hop MDE. At Slice 5, from the measured baseline F1/EM
  variance, draw question-level paired-bootstrap resamples under **≥3 effect shapes** —
  **flat-positive +0.03**, **monotonic** (2<3<4), and **inverted-U** (peaking at 3-hop) — push each
  through `decide()`, and report **P(GO)** for each shape. N (the pooled ≥3-hop cell) is sized so the
  rule attains **rule-level P(GO) ≥ 0.8 under the flat-positive +0.03** shape; `power_ok = True`
  **only if** that holds. The material threshold `MATERIAL_F1_LIFT = 0.02` is **at/above** the
  Slice-5 pooled ≥3-hop MDE (corrected: the threshold must sit *at or above* the MDE, not below it).
  Sampling is `log()`-ed (no silent cap); re-estimated only via the reserved follow-on if baseline
  variance is wider than assumed — the endpoint and rule above do not move.

### 4.1 Decision rule — truth table (frozen as `decide()`)

`decide(material, em, trend, confident_wrong, power_ok)`, where
`material = {"f1_delta", "f1_ci_low"}`, `em = {"ci_high"}`, `trend = {"neg_significant"}`,
`confident_wrong = {"increase_significant"}`. **GO iff every gate below holds; else NO_GO.**

| gate | condition for GO | failure ⇒ |
|---|---|---|
| material | `f1_delta ≥ 0.02` **and** `f1_ci_low > 0` | **NO_GO** (sub-material or CI includes 0) |
| trend | `not neg_significant` (flat/positive OK) | **NO_GO** only on a **significantly negative** slope |
| EM | `ci_high ≥ 0` | **NO_GO** (EM significantly worse — CI-banded) |
| confident-wrong | `not increase_significant` | **NO_GO** (unanswerable-set confident-answer rate up) |
| power | `power_ok` | **NO_GO** (underpowered) |

Non-finite (`NaN`/±inf) `f1_delta`/`f1_ci_low`/`ci_high` ⇒ **`ValueError`** (loud-fail, not a
verdict). Determinism: same input → same verdict (no clock, no RNG, no I/O in `decide()`).

**Load-bearing change vs the old rule:** a **flat-positive** case — equal positive per-hop ΔF1 (so
*not* strictly increasing), pooled ≥3-hop ΔF1 ≥ 0.02 with CI > 0, EM ok, no confident-wrong,
powered — now returns **GO**. The old strict-monotonic gate returned NO_GO here; that false-NO_GO
was the dominant bias the amendment removes.

## 5. Same-answerer protocol

Every arm feeds the **identical answerer** the **identical top-K passage budget**; retrieval is the
only variable. Reuses the `r2_parity_eval.py` invariant: adapters expose only `retrieve(question, k)`
and cannot build a prompt, so per-system prompt divergence is structurally impossible; the harness
owns the single answerer. Greedy/seeded decode; versioned output JSONs (never overwrite —
[[perf-tuning-design-sweeps-not-adhoc]]). Strong reader = **`gemini-3.1-pro-preview`** (env-driven via
`R2_ANSWERER_MODEL` over the Airlock proxy). EM/F1 reuse the `r2_parity_eval.py` normalize/score
primitives (`normalize_answer`, `_normalize`, token-F1).

## 6. Ablations

- **Restart→1.0 collapse** (built-in sanity): at teleport probability 1.0 the PPR arm degenerates to
  seeds ≈ BM25 (Slice-15 RED pins this).
- **IDF-weighting on/off** — changes ranking (knob is live; Slice-15 RED pins this).
- **Fusion no-regression** — RRF never drops a BM25-top-K passage below its BM25-alone floor
  (Slice-15 RED pins this).
- **Reserved (Slice-15 follow-on, run only if PPR-fusion is close to the bar):** entity-seeds vs
  passage-node-seeds granularity; **F9 confidence-weighted seeds/edges** as an *ablation knob only*
  (do not open the F9 slice).

## 7. Bias controls

- **Unanswerable contrast set** — guards against the graph inducing confident wrong answers
  (reported alongside the answerable endpoint, never folded into it).
- **Body-less edges** — Slice-10 builds edges **without `body`** (the opposite of
  `graph_arm_recall.py::build_graph_engine`'s default) to dodge the engine `edge_fact`
  vector-projection scale bug (0.8.1 §7); this is called out explicitly so the graph arm is not
  silently crippled by an engine bug rather than by topology.
- **Same answerer/depth/decode across all arms** (§5) — isolates retrieval as the only variable.
- **No silent caps** — any sampling (question subset, distractor cap, traversal cap) is `log()`-ed in
  the artifact; a truncated run is labelled, not reported as full coverage.
- **Pinned provenance** — every run artifact is pinned to `musique_hash` + extractor model id + arm
  config; never overwrite a prior run JSON.

## 8. Budget plan (the one priced seam)

**Footprint invariant holds:** CPU-only, no-API at the library boundary, 1-bit-safe. Extraction is
the local offline Qwen3.6-27B (Airlock vLLM batch, **$0**). The **answerer is the only priced seam**.

- **Cheap-validate GATE (mandatory before ANY priced run).** Validate field population + harness
  wiring with **`gemini-2.5-flash-lite`** first; only after the cheap pass is green does a priced
  `gemini-3.1-pro-preview` pass run ([[0.8.1-budget-discipline-cheap-validate-and-ledger]]). The
  cheap-validate is run at Slice 5 (baseline e2e) and again at Slice 20 (multi-arm) before the
  priced pass.
- **Call-count model.** Priced answerer calls ≈ **(#arms) × N** per pass (one answerer call per
  question per arm; retrieval/PPR/rerank/extraction are $0). Slice 5 prices the **4 baseline arms**
  ({bm25, passage-dense, fused, fused+rerank}) × N; Slice 20 adds the 5th arm (ppr-fusion) × N. N is
  the Slice-5-chosen power-satisfying sample (§4 mde-power-plan).
- **Running $ ledger** maintained in `dev/plans/runs/STATUS-0.8.2.md` (cheap-validate $ + each
  priced pass $); finalized at Slice 20.

## 11. Decision (frozen)

- **GO → 0.8.3 (M2 full study):** ppr-fusion shows a **material, CI-excludes-0 pooled ≥3-hop F1 lift**
  over the fixed `fused+rerank` comparator, with **no significantly negative hop trend**, **EM not
  significantly worse**, **no confident-wrong increase** on the unanswerable set, at **adequate
  whole-rule power** (`decide() == "GO"`).
- **NO-GO → record the negative, redirect to index-key enrichment:** sub-material/CI-includes-0, or a
  significantly negative trend, or significantly worse EM, or a confident-wrong increase, or
  underpowered ⇒ graph *topology* adds no multi-hop answer signal on this corpus — the strongest fair
  version of the graph hypothesis, refuted. 0.8.3/0.8.4 may still proceed (different structures/axes)
  with a "prior: negative" framing on the graph-traversal arm.

**HITL gate (orchestrator-run, not the implementer):** this design + pre-registration is signed
before any priced run (Slice 20).
