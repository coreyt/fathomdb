# M1 ‚ÄĒ multi-hop answer-accuracy harness: design + pre-registration

> **Purpose.** Freeze, *before any data is seen*, the instrument that fairly adjudicates whether a
> knowledge graph improves multi-hop **answer accuracy** (EM/F1) on MuSiQue. This is step 1 of 3
> (M1/M2/S1). The primary endpoint, hop strata, decision rule, and power plan below are **frozen and
> dated**; the GO/NO-GO computation is additionally frozen as code in
> `src/python/eval/m1_decision_rule.py::decide()` so a downstream slice (Slice 20) cannot post-hoc
> switch the endpoint. `decide()` is imported, never redefined.

## 0. Why this instrument (and why it is fair)

The 0.8.1 "beat BM25" investigation reached a **robust but narrow NEGATIVE**
(`dev/plans/runs/0.8.1-beat-bm25-report.md`; [[graph-arm-doesnt-beat-bm25-pivot]]): it measured a
**raw-BFS** graph arm on **first-stage needle-recall** (the graph-disfavored axis) with
**self-seeding** (the graph's own FTS, not the lexical top-K). It says nothing about multi-hop
**answer accuracy**, the axis where graphs are *claimed* to help, because we never measured it and
lacked the instrument. M1 builds the instrument and tests the *strongest fair* lightweight graph
method, so the verdict ‚ÄĒ expected near-tie to modest loss (GraphRAG-Bench arXiv:2506.05690;
RAG-vs-GraphRAG arXiv:2502.11371) ‚ÄĒ converts *"we tried a weak BFS arm"* into *"we tried the
literature's best lightweight graph method and it still didn't win,"* the result that earns the
index-key-enrichment pivot.

Two corrections from the 0.8.1 post-mortem are baked into the design:
- **Not raw BFS.** Uniform BFS is the documented anti-pattern (subgraph blow-up + hub drift = our
  entity-co-mingling failure). The principled lightweight form is **HippoRAG-style lexically-seeded
  Personalized PageRank fused with BM25** (HippoRAG NeurIPS 2024 arXiv:2405.14831; HippoRAG 2
  arXiv:2502.14802). PPR strictly dominates BFS ‚Üí if PPR-fusion can't beat the baseline, BFS never
  would. Clean adjudication.
- **Strong baseline, not a lexical strawman.** Graph wins vanish against a strong dense baseline, so
  the baseline arm is **BM25 ‚ą™ passage-level dense ‚ą™ fused(RRF)** ‚ÄĒ see ¬ß3.

## 1. Datasets

- **MuSiQue-Ans, distractor setting (~20 paragraphs/question)** ‚ÄĒ the arbiter (MuSiQue, TACL 2022,
  arXiv:2108.00573). Engineered to defeat shortcuts (3√ó the human‚Äďmachine gap; ~30-pt F1 drop for
  single-hop/disconnected models vs prior sets). Two built-in properties make a measured lift
  *attributable to the index*, not to artifacts (the HotpotQA trap):
  - **2/3/4-hop split = a built-in dose-response.** Graph benefit, if real, should *grow* with hop
    count. This is the load-bearing signal (¬ß4).
  - **Unanswerable contrast set** ‚ÄĒ a confident-wrong-answer guard (graph must not induce confident
    wrong answers on the unanswerable set). Secondary, not primary.
- **License/provenance.** CC-BY-4.0 (`StonyBrookNLP/musique`). Acquired by a Slice-5 script,
  **gitignored**, pinned by a `musique_hash`. (Acquire script is **Slice 5**, not this slice.)
- **Deliberately NOT here:** 2WikiMultiHopQA, MultiHop-RAG ‚Üí **0.8.3 (M2)**.

## 2. Arms

| Arm | What it is | Stage |
|---|---|---|
| **bm25** | FTS5/BM25 lexical retrieval (existing) | baseline component |
| **passage-dense** | passage-level embedder (existing; **not** whole-doc R4) | baseline component |
| **fused** | RRF(bm25, passage-dense) ‚ÄĒ the strong baseline's fusion | **strong baseline** |
| **ppr-fusion** | lexically-seeded Personalized PageRank over the per-question graph, RRF-fused with BM25 | the graph arm under test |

`ppr-fusion` mechanism (built in Slice 15, specified here): **(1) Seed** from BM25/FTS5 top-K ‚Üí
map to graph entity nodes; **weight seeds by IDF / node specificity** (FTS5 term stats, free) to
suppress hub drift. **(2) Propagate** one Personalized-PageRank pass biased to those seeds;
deterministic (fixed teleport probability, fixed iteration cap + tolerance, stable node ordering).
**(3) Rank** passages by summed PPR mass; **fuse with BM25 via RRF** (HippoRAG-2's lesson:
graph+lexical fusion beats graph-alone and avoids entity-only context loss).

## 3. The strong-baseline definition (harness requirement, not optional)

> **The baseline is `BM25 ‚ą™ passage-level dense ‚ą™ fused(RRF)` ‚ÄĒ NOT lexical-only.**

Measuring the graph arm against a lexical-only strawman is the methodological error that would
invalidate the verdict. Two constraints:
- **Passage-level dense, not whole-doc (R4).** Whole-doc vectors blur discrimination (our own
  measured failure mode; R4 stays parked in 0.8.5).
- **"Best baseline" for the endpoint** = the per-hop max over {bm25, passage-dense, fused}, so the
  graph arm must beat the strongest of the three at each hop count ‚ÄĒ not merely the weakest.

## 4. Pre-registration (FROZEN 2026-06-16)

> Machine-checked by the schema-lint (`m1_decision_rule.lint_preregistration`) + the
> `test_m1_decision_rule.py` pre-registration test. Each `frozen-field:` line below is required and
> must carry a date; removing or undating any line fails the build.

- **frozen-field: primary-endpoint** (frozen 2026-06-16): the **paired őĒEM / őĒF1 = (ppr-fusion) ‚ąí
  (best baseline)** on the MuSiQue-Ans **answerable** set, reported **per hop count (2/3/4)**, with
  the **2‚Üí3‚Üí4 dose-response load-bearing** (does graph benefit *grow* with hops?). EM and F1 are
  both reported; **F1 is the primary continuous signal** (single-digit-F1 regime per the
  literature), EM is a coarse corroborating guard. The unanswerable-set behavior is a
  confident-wrong guard, **not** a primary endpoint.
- **frozen-field: per-hop-strata** (frozen 2026-06-16): results are **stratified by MuSiQue hop
  count into exactly the three strata {2, 3, 4}**; no pooling across hops for the primary endpoint
  (pooling hides the dose-response). The decision reads the ‚Č•3-hop strata (3 and 4) as the
  graph-favored region; the 2-hop stratum anchors the dose-response baseline.
- **frozen-field: decision-rule** (frozen 2026-06-16): the GO/NO-GO call is computed **mechanically**
  by `src/python/eval/m1_decision_rule.py::decide(deltas_by_hop, power_ok)`; Slice 20 imports it and
  **may not redefine it**. Prose form: **GO** iff the ‚Č•3-hop F1 lift is **material**
  (`F1 ‚Č• MATERIAL_F1_LIFT` on both hop-3 and hop-4), **dose-responsive** (F1 lift strictly grows
  `2 < 3 < 4`), **EM-non-regressing** on the ‚Č•3-hop strata (őĒEM ‚Č• `EM_MIN_LIFT_3PLUS`), **and**
  adequately powered (`power_ok=True`); otherwise **NO_GO**. Frozen thresholds:
  `MATERIAL_F1_LIFT = 0.02` (2 F1 points ‚ÄĒ the smallest ‚Č•3-hop lift worth a GO, sized below Slice-5
  MDE), `EM_MIN_LIFT_3PLUS = 0.0`, `REQUIRED_HOPS = (2, 3, 4)`.
- **frozen-field: mde-power-plan** (frozen 2026-06-16): N is chosen at Slice 5 from the measured
  baseline F1 variance so that the **per-hop MDE < `MATERIAL_F1_LIFT` (0.02)** at őĪ=0.05, power 0.8,
  on a **paired** (same-question, same-answerer) comparison; `power_ok` is set true iff the realized
  per-hop N yields MDE < `MATERIAL_F1_LIFT` on **each** of the 2/3/4 strata. Because graph multi-hop
  wins are single-digit F1, N must be non-trivial; Slice 5 samples the MuSiQue dev set accordingly
  and `log()`s the sampling (no silent cap). The MDE/power numbers are **estimated at Slice 5 and
  re-estimated only via the reserved follow-on if baseline variance is wider than assumed** ‚ÄĒ the
  endpoint and rule above do not move.

### 4.1 Decision rule ‚ÄĒ truth table (frozen as `decide()`)

| ‚Č•3-hop F1 lift | dose-responsive (2<3<4) | őĒEM ‚Č•3-hop | power_ok | verdict |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| flat or negative | ‚ÄĒ | ‚ÄĒ | ‚ÄĒ | **NO_GO** |
| ‚Č• material, both 3 & 4 | no (flat/decreasing) | ‚ÄĒ | ‚ÄĒ | **NO_GO** |
| ‚Č• material | yes | ‚Č• 0 | **False** | **NO_GO** (underpowered) |
| ‚Č• material | yes | ‚Č• 0 | **True** | **GO** |
| ‚Č• material | yes | **< 0** (EM regresses) | ‚ÄĒ | **NO_GO** (confident-wrong guard) |

Determinism: same input ‚Üí same verdict (no clock, no RNG, no I/O in `decide()`).

## 5. Same-answerer protocol

Every arm feeds the **identical answerer** the **identical top-K passage budget**; retrieval is the
only variable. Reuses the `r2_parity_eval.py` invariant: adapters expose only `retrieve(question, k)`
and cannot build a prompt, so per-system prompt divergence is structurally impossible; the harness
owns the single answerer. Greedy/seeded decode; versioned output JSONs (never overwrite ‚ÄĒ
[[perf-tuning-design-sweeps-not-adhoc]]). Strong reader = **`gemini-3.1-pro-preview`** (env-driven via
`R2_ANSWERER_MODEL` over the Airlock proxy). EM/F1 reuse the `r2_parity_eval.py` normalize/score
primitives (`normalize_answer`, `_normalize`, token-F1).

## 6. Ablations

- **Restart‚Üí1.0 collapse** (built-in sanity): at teleport probability 1.0 the PPR arm degenerates to
  seeds ‚Čą BM25 (Slice-15 RED pins this).
- **IDF-weighting on/off** ‚ÄĒ changes ranking (knob is live; Slice-15 RED pins this).
- **Fusion no-regression** ‚ÄĒ RRF never drops a BM25-top-K passage below its BM25-alone floor
  (Slice-15 RED pins this).
- **Reserved (Slice-15 follow-on, run only if PPR-fusion is close to the bar):** entity-seeds vs
  passage-node-seeds granularity; **F9 confidence-weighted seeds/edges** as an *ablation knob only*
  (do not open the F9 slice).

## 7. Bias controls

- **Unanswerable contrast set** ‚ÄĒ guards against the graph inducing confident wrong answers
  (reported alongside the answerable endpoint, never folded into it).
- **Body-less edges** ‚ÄĒ Slice-10 builds edges **without `body`** (the opposite of
  `graph_arm_recall.py::build_graph_engine`'s default) to dodge the engine `edge_fact`
  vector-projection scale bug (0.8.1 ¬ß7); this is called out explicitly so the graph arm is not
  silently crippled by an engine bug rather than by topology.
- **Same answerer/depth/decode across all arms** (¬ß5) ‚ÄĒ isolates retrieval as the only variable.
- **No silent caps** ‚ÄĒ any sampling (question subset, distractor cap, traversal cap) is `log()`-ed in
  the artifact; a truncated run is labelled, not reported as full coverage.
- **Pinned provenance** ‚ÄĒ every run artifact is pinned to `musique_hash` + extractor model id + arm
  config; never overwrite a prior run JSON.

## 8. Budget plan (the one priced seam)

**Footprint invariant holds:** CPU-only, no-API at the library boundary, 1-bit-safe. Extraction is
the local offline Qwen3.6-27B (Airlock vLLM batch, **$0**). The **answerer is the only priced seam**.

- **Cheap-validate GATE (mandatory before ANY priced run).** Validate field population + harness
  wiring with **`gemini-2.5-flash-lite`** first; only after the cheap pass is green does a priced
  `gemini-3.1-pro-preview` pass run ([[0.8.1-budget-discipline-cheap-validate-and-ledger]]). The
  cheap-validate is run at Slice 5 (baseline e2e) and again at Slice 20 (multi-arm) before the
  priced pass.
- **Call-count model.** Priced answerer calls ‚Čą **(#arms) √ó N** per pass (one answerer call per
  question per arm; retrieval/PPR/extraction are $0). Slice 5 prices 3 baseline arms √ó N; Slice 20
  adds the 4th arm (ppr-fusion) √ó N. N is the Slice-5-chosen MDE-satisfying sample (¬ß4
  mde-power-plan).
- **Running $ ledger** maintained in `dev/plans/runs/STATUS-0.8.2.md` (cheap-validate $ + each
  priced pass $); finalized at Slice 20.

## 10. Falsifiable AC list (the Slice 5 / 15 / 20 bars)

Each downstream slice tests against a falsifiable bar; these are the M1 acceptance bars this design
commits to.

**Slice 5 ‚ÄĒ MuSiQue corpus + strong baseline + answerer e2e (THE BAR):**
- **AC-M1-5a** ‚ÄĒ the retrieval+answer pipeline runs end-to-end over the pinned `musique_hash` and

- **AC-M1-5b** ‚ÄĒ EM/F1 are reported **stratified by hop count {2,3,4}** (no pooled-only number) plus

## 11. Decision (frozen)

- **GO ‚Üí 0.8.3 (M2 full study):** ppr-fusion shows a material, dose-responsive ‚Č•3-hop EM/F1 lift over
  the strong baseline at adequate power (`decide() == "GO"`).
- **NO-GO ‚Üí record the negative, redirect to index-key enrichment:** flat-or-negative or
  non-dose-responsive or underpowered ‚áí graph *topology* adds no multi-hop answer signal on this
  corpus ‚ÄĒ the strongest fair version of the graph hypothesis, refuted. 0.8.3/0.8.4 may still proceed
  (different structures/axes) with a "prior: negative" framing on the graph-traversal arm.
