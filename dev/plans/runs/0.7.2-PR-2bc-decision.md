## Premise (settled; not re-argued here)

The EU-7 "recall gap" (0.828 < 0.90 floor) was root-caused as a MEASUREMENT
ARTIFACT, not an engine/mean/embedder defect:

- Corrected ANN recall@10 = **0.937** (CI 0.913-0.957); full CI clears the 0.90
  floor. +7.8pp over the old harness number was pure measurement methodology.
- The mean is a **non-lever**: forcing the full-corpus mean on the real engine
  gives 0.847, only +1.9pp over the worst pin; `drift_cos_before = 1.0000` showed
  PR-2b had ALREADY converged the pinned mean to the true full-corpus mean during
  seeding.
- candle vs HF embeddings are bit-identical. IR recall 0.571 (embedder semantic
  ceiling) << ANN 0.937 -> the quantization is not the bottleneck.

So both PR-2b and PR-2c were built to solve a problem that does not exist for
recall. The question below is decided on NON-recall merits only.

## 1. PR-2c -- representative initial pin: SHELVE (confirm the recommendation)

**Verdict: SHELVE** (revert to compute-once-pin-at-256). The reassessment
recommendation is CONFIRMED, and on a stronger basis than "recall-neutral": PR-2c
is recall-NEGATIVE for the most common workspace size.

Non-recall (and recall-cost) reasoning:

1. **No recall upside.** The mean moves real recall +1.9pp at most; recall was
   never broken. PR-2c's entire justification was the single-source-pin story
   (cos 0.82, -10.9pp) which was an offline artifact that does not transfer.
2. **It is recall-NEGATIVE for typical workspaces.** PR-2c extends the un-centered
   cold-start window from `[0, 256)` to `[0, 2048)` (settle threshold 2048). EU-0
   measured the un-centered path at ~-5pp recall@10. embedder.md §0.3 states most
   realistic agentic-memory workspaces are "100s-1000s of docs total" -- i.e. a
   large fraction of real workspaces would live entirely or mostly inside the
   longer un-centered window. PR-2c therefore trades a non-existent benefit for a
   real regression on exactly the workspace sizes we target.
3. **It reopens a deliberately-locked constant.** embedder.md §0.3 locks
   `MEAN_VEC_PIN_THRESHOLD = 256` with an explicit statistical + operational
   justification and the note "the choice is not load-bearing to within a factor
   of 2 ... but it is locked here." PR-2c changes WHEN the pin fires (256 -> 2048),
   reopening that lock for no measured gain.
4. **It is redundant with PR-2b.** Even granting that pin representativeness
   mattered, the root-cause run showed PR-2b's recompute ALREADY drives the pinned
   mean to the true full-corpus mean during seeding (`drift_cos_before = 1.0000`).
   The initial-pin representativeness PR-2c buys is subsumed by the recompute.
5. **Added standing state for nothing.** `MeanReservoir` (Algorithm R, seeded) +
   `MEAN_PIN_SETTLE_THRESHOLD` + deferred-pin logic in both pin sites is permanent
   engine surface whose only purpose was the disproven recall fix.

PR-2c is NOT on `main` (branch `0.7.2-PR-2c-representative-pin`, `e8d5538`), so
"shelve" requires no revert on main -- just do not merge it. Park the branch with
a pointer to this memo. The ANN harness fix that lives on top of it (`a601d1c`)
lands independently (see section 3).

## 2. PR-2b -- production mean recompute: KEEP core + verb, MODIFY out the auto-detector

Split the slice into two parts and decide them separately:

### 2a. Recompute core + `doctor recompute-mean` verb -- KEEP

The user explicitly wanted a production recompute capability INDEPENDENT of recall
("I do want the recompute mean function for production"). This stands on its own
non-recall merits:

- It addresses the **genuine, documented topic-drift failure mode** (§0.3): a
  workspace that pins on topic A then pivots to topic B. That failure is real even
  though it was not the EU-7 cause.
- The manual verb is **operator-invoked only** -- zero standing cost on the hot
  path, no speculative complexity. It is a clean tool an operator reaches for when
  they observe drift.
- It reuses the proven `run_pin_and_requantize_pass` (no forked re-quantize),
  inherits the §0.5 crash-atomicity guarantees, and is already GREEN + tested
  (`tests/pr2b_mean_recompute.rs`).

**KEEP** the `recompute_mean_in_tx` core, the `doctor recompute-mean` verb (incl.
the normative `--json` contract), and the `MeanVecRecomputed { trigger: Manual }`
event.

### 2b. Automatic in-ingest drift detector + 200k cap -- MODIFY (carve out / defer)

**Recommendation: defer the automatic path.** Reasoning on non-recall merits:

- **Its benefit is unmeasured.** The only evidence it works is a SYNTHETIC
  topic-drift efficacy test. There is no production or real-corpus signal that
  auto-drift firing improves anything users see -- and the recall premise that
  justified building it has collapsed (mean is a non-lever).
- **It is permanent complexity on the hottest path.** Per-commit EWMA
  (`alpha = 1/256`) maintenance, a `cos(recent, pinned)` check every commit, the
  falling-edge re-arm latch + `MEAN_RECOMPUTE_DEBOUNCE_ROWS` debounce, the
  `MEAN_RECOMPUTE_DYNAMIC_MAX = 200_000` cap branch, and the
  `MeanRecomputeDeferred` event plumbing -- all live in `commit_projection_outcomes`
  forever, for a speculative trigger.
- **Its ratification is now unmoored.** HITL ratified the drift policy
  (0.95 / debounce 256 / EWMA 1/256) on 2026-05-30 under the belief it helped
  recall. That belief is gone; the calibrated numbers were never validated against
  a real drift event, only chosen to sit in the 0.82-0.998 gap PR-2a reported.
- **The manual verb already gives operators the lever.** An operator who observes
  drift runs `doctor recompute-mean`. Auto-firing inside ingest is the part that
  earns the least and costs the most.

**MODIFY = ship 2a now; carve 2b (auto-detector + 200k cap + `MeanRecomputeDeferred`)
out into a parked, ready-to-revive follow-up slice** ("PR-2b-auto"), to be revived
only when there is a measured production/real-corpus drift case that the manual
verb demonstrably fails to cover.

Caveat for HITL: PR-2b (both parts) is ALREADY on local `main` (`b96c850` feat +
`e187add` ratify), unpushed. So "carve out 2b" is itself a small engine edit
(RED-first, HITL-gated) against main -- it is NOT free. If HITL prefers, the
alternative is **KEEP 2b as-is** (it is built, GREEN, ratified, and the per-row
cost is genuinely O(dim)) and simply re-document its rationale as "topic-drift
safety, not recall." This is a close call and a legitimate HITL choice; my
recommendation is the carve-out because shipping permanently-on speculative
hot-path machinery, whose sole original justification has been refuted, is the
higher long-term cost. Either way: **no engine change until HITL rules.**

## 4. PR-2 reframe -- floor HOLDS at 0.90

With corrected ANN recall 0.937 (CI-low 0.913, sigma 0.0116), PR-2's job is no
longer "lower the floor." Reframe:

1. **Land the harness fix** (section 3) so `eu7_real_corpus_ac.rs` measures the
   gate the standard ANN way.
2. **Keep `AC013B_RECALL_FLOOR = 0.90`.** A mechanical `R - 2*sigma` derivation now
   gives `0.937 - 0.0232 = 0.914 -> 0.91`, which is ABOVE the current 0.90 floor.
   So 0.90 is comfortably defensible and conservative; do NOT raise it to 0.91
   (that would spend the headroom we want for production variance, and the N=1M
   canonical run (PR-3) is still pending). Keep 0.90; document 0.937 as the anchor
   with ~3.7pp point / ~1.4pp CI-low margin.
3. **Amend the ADR** (`ADR-0.7.0-vector-binary-quant.md §2 point 4`) to cite the
   corrected 0.937 measurement AND the harness-methodology finding (exclude-before
   vs exclude-after; dedup-GT vs body-string GT), so the floor's provenance is the
   correct number, not the artifact 0.828. Keep the existing sentinel test
   (`ac_013b_floor_matches_adr`).
4. **Record the EU-8 IR ceiling (0.571) as a separate story** in the ADR/notes: it
   is the embedder/task ceiling, not a quantization gate; future work is
   semantic/graph, NOT K/ANN tuning.

This keeps faith with the carried-forward hard constraint "do not re-word ADRs to
retcon a failure into a pass" -- here the honest move is the reverse: the number
was always fine; we are correcting the ADR to cite the right measurement.
