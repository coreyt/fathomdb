# Paper

## Goal

FathomDB is a specialized, embedded, high-performing database built to be the memory store for personal agents. Its product thesis is that a single local SQLite-backed engine can serve agent memory end-to-end — store long multi-session histories, retrieve the right evidence, and feed a reader to a correct answer — at parity with dedicated agent-memory systems while remaining embeddable and fast. This report adjudicates that thesis across seven cleaned capabilities: (1) **Agentic long-term memory** (end-to-end competitive parity vs Mem0/Zep under an identical-answerer protocol); (2) **Information-retrieval relevance** (first-stage retrieval & ranking vs a strong BM25 baseline); (3) **Exploratory retrieval** (graph-augmented & index-key-enrichment lift on cross-session discovery); (4) **Deep / multi-hop retrieval & reasoning** (graph bridging on explicit 2/3/4-hop QA); (5) **Answer synthesis** (end-to-end generative QA and the reader bottleneck); (6) **Retrieval fidelity & embedding integrity** (ANN/quantization recall vs exact, and embed-completeness — system health, not relevance); and (7) **Engine performance** (read/scan latency & concurrency — the "high-performing embedded database" claim).

## Capabilities

**1. Agentic long-term memory.** As a drop-in personal-agent memory store, does FathomDB recall and answer over long, multi-session histories at least as well as naive-RAG and Mem0/Zep, under a strictly identical-answerer protocol (one answerer, one prompt, one context budget — so any gap is retrieval, not prompt divergence)?
- *Measures:* strict full-gold-set Recall@K (all `gold_doc_ids` in top-K); identical-answerer answer accuracy (normalized substring `_match`, abstention-scored); per-class deltas `fathomdb_minus_{mem0,naive_rag}`; abstention_rate.

**2. Information-retrieval relevance (first-stage retrieval & ranking).** On single/shallow-hop questions, is FathomDB's base retrieval and ranking at or above a strong lexical (BM25) floor, measured LLM-free, before any graph/multi-hop work? Levers: FTS5, dense BGE bi-encoder, RRF hybrid fusion, CPU cross-encoder rerank.
- *Measures:* pooled & per-class Recall@5/10/20 (abstention-excluded); MRR; nDCG@10; recall delta vs BM25 / prior slice against a paired MDE.

**3. Exploratory retrieval (graph-augmented & enrichment lift).** Do two structural/content levers — an LLM-extracted entity/edge graph with BFS traversal, and index-key enrichment (appending a session's own extracted entities/facts to its FTS body) — beat plain lexical retrieval on cross-session discovery (LongMemEval `multi_session`)? Includes placebo and BM25 length-norm (`b`) controls.
- *Measures:* pooled & per-class Recall@10 (`multi_session` = full-gold-set issubset); graph ON-vs-OFF same-engine paired A/B delta; enriched-minus-plain and length-matched placebo delta; BM25 `b`-sweep curve; MRR, nDCG@10.

**4. Deep / multi-hop retrieval & reasoning.** Can a lexically-seeded Personalized-PageRank graph-fusion arm genuinely bridge across documents on explicit 2/3/4-hop QA (MuSiQue-Answerable) and lift ≥3-hop answer accuracy above the strongest fused-RRF baseline?
- *Measures:* pooled ≥3-hop F1 + EM; paired-bootstrap 95% CI on ΔF1 vs MATERIAL_F1_LIFT=0.04; per-hop ΔF1 and OLS hop-trend slope; gets-all-bridges@10 and F1 | all-bridges-present.

**5. Answer synthesis (end-to-end generative QA).** Does retrieved context pass faithfully through a reader to a correct answer, and is the reader (vs retrieval) the binding constraint? Isolated by holding retrieval fixed and swapping readers.
- *Measures:* judged answer accuracy (LLM judge) and normalized-substring accuracy, per-class + overall; answer F1; reader-swap delta on identical retrieval; n_answered / abstention rate.

**6. Retrieval fidelity & embedding integrity.** System health of the vector stack (distinct from IR relevance): how faithfully 1-bit sign-quantized ANN + f32 rerank reproduces the exact-f32 top-K (recall-floor gated), and whether a dense-embed DB is complete before fused retrieval is trusted.
- *Measures:* ANN recall@10 vs exact-f32 GT with one-sided 95% CI floor (0.90); per-kind embed coverage = n_docs_embedded / n_docs (vectors JOINed to live nodes); six boolean completeness checks.

**7. Engine performance (read/scan latency & concurrency).** The embedded engine's raw operational performance under stacked SQLite/engine levers — the "high-performing embedded database" claim, orthogonal to retrieval quality.
- *Measures:* AC012 single-read p50/p99 @ n=1M; AC013 scan p50/p99 @ n=100k; AC020 sequential-vs-concurrent latency and speedup ratio.

**R2 memory-class go/no-go (Slice 25) — same frozen corpus.** The four memory classes that define the capability (temporal, multi_hop, knowledge_update, multi_session) had **N=0 gold**, so every memory-class delta was null → **DATA-LIMITED, HITL escalation**.

*Most successful approach to date:* none has won — and as of **0.8.3 D0b (2026-06-22) the headline parity number now EXISTS** (clears this report's #1 caveat). (Earlier Slice-25 state — N=0 gold, answerer/Mem0 unavailable — is superseded by the D0b re-pin + stand-up.) (See Appendix → Agentic long-term memory + `runs/0.8.3-report.md`.)

### 3. Exploratory retrieval (graph-augmented & enrichment lift)

**Exp 1 — Graph-arm BFS recall sweep, ON vs OFF same-engine A/B (LongMemEval, n=40; 1,907 sessions, 28,883 entities / 38,021 edges, Qwen3.6-27B extraction, $0).**

| Arm | R@10 | multi_session | MRR | nDCG@10 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **fathomdb_fts_only (docs-only FTS5)** | **0.80** | **0.30** | **0.640** | **0.633** |
| naive_bm25 | 0.70 | 0.30 | 0.701 | 0.658 |
| graph_OFF (entities in index, BFS off) | 0.65 | 0.30 | 0.489 | 0.468 |
| graph_ON (BFS traversal on) | 0.65 | 0.10 | 0.512 | 0.636 |

The BFS arm adds **exactly 0.00** pooled R@10 (ON == OFF = 0.65) and *degrades* its own target class (multi_session 0.30 → 0.10). Co-mingling entity rows costs −0.15 vs docs-only FTS. Clear negative.

**Exp 2 — R6 index-key enrichment + length-matched placebo (LongMemEval, n=40, cached graphs, $0).**

| Arm | R@10 | Δ vs plain |
|---|---|---|
| **fathomdb_fts_only (plain docs)** | **0.800** | — |
| fathomdb_fts_enriched (primary) | 0.775 | −0.025 |
| naive_bm25_enriched | 0.750 | (+0.05 vs bm25) |
| naive_bm25 | 0.700 | — |
| fathomdb_fts_placebo (length-matched filler) | 0.700 | −0.100 |

Enrichment did **not** beat plain FTS (0.775 < 0.800). The placebo decomposes why: length-matched contentless filler drops −0.10 (a real FTS5 length-norm penalty), real content recovers +0.075 — genuine content value, but insufficient to clear the docs-only bar.

**Exp 3 — BM25 b-parameter sweep × {plain, enriched} (LongMemEval, n=40; b ∈ {0, 0.25, 0.5, 0.75}).**

| Arm | R@10 |
|---|---|
| **fathomdb_fts_only (FTS5, fixed b)** | **0.800** |
| bm25_enriched b=0.0 | 0.775 |
| bm25_enriched b=0.25 | 0.775 |
| bm25_plain b=0.0 | 0.750 |
| bm25_enriched b=0.5 | 0.750 |
| bm25_enriched b=0.75 | 0.750 |
| bm25_plain b=0.25 | 0.725 |
| bm25_plain b=0.5 | 0.725 |
| bm25_plain b=0.75 | 0.700 |

Lower b monotonically lifts recall for both plain and enriched, and enrichment is additive at every b — confirming the length-norm mechanism behind Exp 1 and 2. The best enriched config (0.775) still trails FathomDB-FTS5 (0.800, fixed/un-exposed b).

*Most successful approach to date:* plain docs-only FathomDB FTS5 (R@10 = 0.80). Neither graph BFS nor index-key enrichment beats it; the only consistently winning configuration is the lexical baseline with no graph and no entity rows in the index. (See Appendix → Exploratory retrieval for why the graph arm is a *lower bound* not a wiring failure, the 3-way enrichment decomposition, and literature corroboration.)

*Next work:* [suggested] expose/lower FTS5's `b` and pair with **fielded** enrichment (BM25F-style sidecar, not blind body-append) — the one untested upside, since the +0.075 placebo-isolated content value is real (needs engine work); [planned] graph-traversal *retrieval* is retired (HITL-signed pivot 2026-06-16, re-confirmed NO-GO in 0.8.2 M1) — graph ships as substrate only; index-key enrichment carried to 0.8.3 D2 (registered, scaled, fielded, with the length-norm fix); GraphRAG global-sensemaking (0.8.4 S1) is the sole surviving graph hypothesis, on a different axis.

### 4. Deep / multi-hop retrieval & reasoning

**Exp 1 — M1 bridge-vs-answer comparator-selection diagnostic ($0, MuSiQue-Answerable, 300 q-arm pairs).** Score = gets-all-bridges@10 / F1-when-all-bridges-present.

| Arm | gets-all-bridges@10 | F1 \| all bridges present |
|---|---|---|
| **fused-RRF (BM25+dense, k=60)** | 0.65 | **0.552** |
| fused_rerank (cross-encoder) | 0.64 | 0.526 |
| bm25 | 0.51 | 0.498 |
| passage_dense | **0.68** | 0.464 |

Complete-bridge retrieval is the dominant answer driver — all-bridges@10 → F1 0.510 vs any-missing → 0.068 (+0.442, ~7×). Dense retrieves the full bridge set most often yet answers worst (buries bridges among similar distractors); fused-RRF composes the cleanest context → **comparator locked to fused-RRF**.

**Exp 2 — M1 priced 5-arm adjudication (gpt-5.4 reader, MuSiQue-Answerable distractor, n=300; 144 ≥3-hop).**

| Arm | ≥3-hop F1 | ≥3-hop EM |
|---|---|---|
| **passage_dense (BGE bi-encoder, CLS)** | **0.4866** | **0.3681** |
| fused-RRF (k=60, comparator) | 0.4502 | 0.3542 |
| fused_rerank (TinyBERT-L2 CE) | 0.4152 | 0.3125 |
| ppr_fusion (PPR α=0.85, lexically seeded, treatment) | 0.4097 | 0.3194 |
| bm25 | 0.3700 | 0.2778 |

**NO-GO (robust).** The graph treatment ppr_fusion lands below the comparator (ΔF1 = −0.0405, 95% CI [−0.116, +0.031]); the CI upper bound (+0.031) is below the +0.04 materiality bar, so a material graph win is ruled out even optimistically. Per-hop ΔF1 is uniformly negative (2h −0.016, 3h −0.044, 4h −0.033), hop-trend slope −0.013 — no dose-response where the hypothesis predicted its edge. The multi-hop signal lived in *dense retrieval* (passage_dense 0.487 > fused 0.450), not graph structure.

*Most successful approach to date:* **passage_dense** (BGE bi-encoder, CLS-pooled) — single strongest arm on ≥3-hop F1 and EM, beating fused-RRF by ~0.036 F1. Nuance: dense *retrieves* bridges most often but *composes* context worst (F1|all-bridges 0.464 vs fused 0.552), so its lead is real but its composition is a known weakness. (See Appendix → Deep/multi-hop for the pre-registration, the load-bearing effect-size-vs-power read, run integrity, and cross-reader robustness.)

*Next work:* [planned] no M1 stage-2 (a clear non-win needs no added N); M2 full graph study DROPPED; 0.8.3 D1 promotes passage_dense to a registered comparator (with the Mean→CLS fix), D2 revives index-key enrichment in fielded BM25F form; GraphRAG sensemaking (0.8.4 S1) survives on a different axis; [suggested] D1 should target bridge *composition* not just presence, and re-pre-register an MDE-feasible materiality/N.

### 5. Answer synthesis (end-to-end generative QA)

**Exp 1 — Base-retrieval end-to-end answer accuracy (LongMemEval, n=160, gemini-3.1-pro answerer, gemini-3.5-flash judge).**

| Arm | Overall | factoid | KU | multi_session | temporal | n_answered |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **naive_bm25** | **0.406** | 0.675 | 0.475 | 0.250 | 0.225 | 94 |
| fathomdb_fused (dense+FTS, RRF) | 0.388 | 0.675 | 0.500 | 0.200 | 0.175 | 95 |
| fathomdb_fts_only | 0.350 | 0.700 | 0.350 | 0.150 | 0.200 | 89 |

The end-to-end fused−BM25 delta is **−0.019**, numerically identical to the recall@10 fused−BM25 delta (−0.019, from 0.606 vs 0.625). Answer accuracy mirrors recall exactly — the reader is a faithful pass-through; no arm that fails on recall is rescued downstream.

**Exp 2 — Reader swap on identical retrieval (LongMemEval, n=160 paired, LLM-judged).**

| Reader | acc (BM25 retrieval) | acc (FTS retrieval) | answering behavior |
|---|---|---|---|
| **gemini-3.1-pro** | **0.406** | **0.350** | answers ~58% |
| gpt-5.4-nano | 0.081 | 0.069 | abstains ~91% (27/320 answered) |

Swapping nano → gemini-3.1-pro lifted FTS accuracy 0.069 → 0.350 (+0.281; BM25 0.081 → 0.406, +0.325) — ~5×. A weak reader is unusable as a headline (mass abstention); a capable reader is required for usable accuracy.

*Most successful approach to date:* a strong reader (gemini-3.1-pro) over a strong lexical retriever (naive BM25): overall accuracy 0.406, top arm in both experiments. The load-bearing conclusion — retrieval (recall) is the binding constraint and is faithfully transmitted, *provided* the reader is capable; weak readers collapse the pipeline regardless of retrieval quality. (See Appendix → Answer synthesis for why the −0.019 correspondence is load-bearing, the abstention accounting, and the reasoning-model max_tokens trap.)

*Next work:* [planned] use LLM-free recall@K as the cheap proxy for the rest of 0.8.1 (justified by the 1:1 mapping), re-anchor all gates to gemini-3.1-pro; [planned] downstream graph/dense levers (0.8.2 M1, 0.8.3 D1) target recall directly; [suggested] none beyond — the reader confound is considered resolved (nano is not a valid headline reader).

### 6. Retrieval fidelity & embedding integrity

**Exp 1 — eu7 real-embedder ANN vector-stage recall fidelity gate (AC-075, Slice 40 / GA), N=7667.**

| Arm | recall@10 | Note |
|---|---|---|
| **ANN vector-stage (1-bit sign-quant K=192 + f32 rerank)** | **0.8960** (CI [0.864, 0.925]) | the fidelity number; floor 0.90 |
| Fused `search()` (RRF hybrid vs vector-only GT) | 0.8710 | fusion divergence, NOT a fidelity number |
| 0.7.x anchor (pre-corpus-expansion) | 0.937 | inferred/conflated, never measured on this corpus |

Measured fidelity 0.896 sits just below the un-lowered 0.90 floor; GA shipped because the one-sided CI upper bound (0.925) clears 0.90 (HITL-accepted). The 0.896-vs-0.937 gap on a byte-identical corpus is a real ~4-point ANN-fidelity regression, not a corpus artifact.

**Exp 2 — verify_embed_db.py per-kind vector-attribution completeness verifier (two real failure DBs, 19,195 docs each).**

| Approach | 0%-coverage DB | ~7%-coverage DB |
|---|---|---|
| **Per-kind vector attribution (vectors JOINed to live nodes)** | **DETECTS** (kind `doc` never registered → 0 doc vectors) | **DETECTS** (1,408 vectors, all `edge_fact`, ZERO doc) |
| Naive `drain()`-returns / projection-terminal count | FALSE-PASS | FALSE-PASS |

Only attribution-via-JOIN is ground truth — drain/terminal counts prove the queue ran, not that a vector was written.

*Most successful approach to date:* per-kind vector attribution joined to live canonical nodes — the only signal that reliably distinguishes a real embed from a queue that ran but produced no doc vectors, catching two real partial-embed DBs naive checks passed. On the fidelity axis, the eu7 one-sided-CI floor gate is the accepted mechanism. (See Appendix → Retrieval fidelity for the floor statistics, why 0.871 is not the fidelity number, the WAL trap, and the vacuous ac_013b caveat.)

*Next work:* [planned] diagnose the ~4-point 0.7.x→0.8.0 fidelity regression via an engine-version A/B bisect (measurement-only first; re-pointed to 0.8.5); [planned] use verify_embed_db as a resumable post-drain GATE in the fused build path (serve fused recall only at coverage == 1.0); [suggested] close the vector-kind binding gap (no production Python surface registers a node vector kind; fused path relies on the `_configure_vector_kind_for_test` seam).

## Conclusion — FathomDB vs competitors, by capability

The honest headline: on its own internal endpoints FathomDB's first-stage retrieval **ties** a strong BM25 baseline (fused-RRF) and its graph/multi-hop levers are **refuted** on both recall and answer-F1; against external agent-memory and multi-hop systems, the comparison is **not apples-to-apples** because the defining end-to-end numbers (identical-answerer accuracy vs Mem0/Zep) were never measured. Three structural caveats govern every cross-vendor row below: (1) LongMemEval/LOCOMO scores swing 15–30 pts with the reader model (GPT-4o vs mini vs GPT-5-mini) and the judge protocol, so cross-vendor numbers are not directly comparable; (2) several competitor scores (Mem0 2026 94.4/92.5, Mastra 94.87%, TiMem 76.88%) are vendor/aggregator-reported secondary sources, not verified leaderboards; (3) FathomDB's gated 0.90/0.937 figure measures ANN **fidelity**, not IR relevance — it must never be quoted as a relevance comparator.

**IR relevance (first-stage retrieval).**

|---|---|---|
| Hybrid (BM25+dense, RRF) | BEIR nDCG@10 | ~2–5% over best single method (production standard) |

FathomDB is **at the strong-baseline floor, not above it**.

Not apples-to-apples (different reader, different MuSiQue cut, different metric basis). The structurally important finding: where HippoRAG-2's KG+PPR **beats** a dense baseline on MuSiQue (+7.1 F1), FathomDB's lexically-seeded PPR **loses** to its own dense/fused arm. FathomDB's graph multi-hop is refuted on both axes (0.8.1 recall, 0.8.2 answer F1); its carried-forward lever is dense, matching the dense-baseline class rather than the graph-SOTA class.

**Answer synthesis (reader bottleneck).** FathomDB has no cross-vendor head-to-head, but its internal finding — a strong reader (gemini-3.1-pro) is ~5× a weak one on identical retrieval — directly corroborates the published reader-sensitivity caveat and the LongMemEval full-context/oracle ceiling (60.2%/82.4%): end-to-end accuracy is reader-bound, so any FathomDB-vs-Mem0/Zep claim is meaningless without fixing the answerer.

**Retrieval fidelity & engine performance.** No external comparators apply: fidelity (ANN vs exact-f32) is a system-health metric specific to FathomDB's quantized stack, and the engine-performance numbers are embedded-SQLite operational latencies with no published competitor baseline. Both are reported as internal best-measured values with PENDING budget adjudication, not competitive positions.

**Net.** FathomDB today is a credible, fast embedded retrieval substrate that **ties a strong lexical baseline** on first-stage relevance and transmits retrieval faithfully to a capable reader. It does **not** yet have the end-to-end agent-memory accuracy number that would let it stand against Mem0/Zep, and its graph/multi-hop differentiators are refuted. The carried-forward bet is dense retrieval quality (D1) plus fielded enrichment (D2) — not graph traversal.

## Agentic long-term memory

**Corpus pinning (COR-2 gate).** `R2Harness.__init__` raises unless `corpus_hash` starts with `fe973fcd` *before* producing any number — it refuses to score an unpinned corpus. `from_queries()` is the documented escape hatch for LongMemEval (`--lme-*`), bypassing the COR-2 pin. R2 runs on the frozen artifact (replay determinism), with no live-binding rebuild.

**Identical-answerer invariant (why the protocol is load-bearing).** Adapters expose only `retrieve(question, k)`; all answerers (`Stub/Recording/Null/LLM/Airlock`) share `BaseAnswerer.PROMPT_TEMPLATE` and one context budget, so per-system prompt divergence is *structurally* impossible. FathomDB makes zero network calls (local SQLite); the answerer LLM and Mem0-OSS are gated test-infra, which is exactly why both were "unavailable" rather than "scored low" — their absence yields `null`, not 0.

**Metric-definition math.**
- Recall@K in `R2Harness.run` is strict all-gold-in-topk: `all(g in retrieved for g in gold)` — not any-hit.
- `PerClassScorer` handles the negative/abstention class: answering a negative is a false positive scored 0.0; a correct abstention scores 1.0. `normalize_answer` maps `"I don't know"`/`idk`/empty → `None` via the shared `_ABSTAIN` set (consistent across sync and batch paths).
- Doc-id resolution: `FathomDBAdapter` maps `SearchHit → corpus doc` via `_make_doc_id_of`, preferring `hit.source_id` canonicalized by `session_id_of` (which strips the `#c<N>` chunk suffix), else a cursor map. `NaiveRAGAdapter` is pure-Python BM25 (inverted index, k1=1.5, b=0.75, idf = log(1+(N−n+0.5)/(n+0.5))).

**Why accuracy is null, not low.** The primary delta in this capability is *answer accuracy*; with the answerer LLM unavailable, only Evidence Recall@K is comparable. The factoid Δ of +0.0017 is far inside any plausible MDE at this gold count, so "tie," not "win." (Cross-reference: the base-retrieval n=160 study found per-class MDE ~21pp and pooled ~11pp on the analogous LongMemEval classes, and a strong reader scored ~5× a nano reader on identical retrieval — i.e. e2e accuracy here would be reader-bottlenecked, which is precisely why the answerer choice matters before any Mem0/Zep claim.)

## Information-retrieval relevance (first-stage retrieval & ranking)

**Design, power, and the "tie not loss" call (Experiment 1).** Paired design, seed 20260614, 4 classes (factoid/knowledge_update/multi_session/temporal). Pooled MDE ≈ 11pp; per-class MDE ≈ 21pp at n=160. The fused-vs-BM25 R@10 delta of −0.019 is well inside the pooled MDE, so it is reported as a tie, not a loss. The fused-vs-FTS-only +0.044 is the isolated dense contribution. `multi_session` (0.325 fused vs 0.275 BM25) is the *only* class where fused exceeds BM25; on ranking quality fused trails BM25 by MRR −0.088 and nDCG −0.064, i.e. dense recovers recall but not rank position.

**Pass-through claim (Experiment 2).** Two source files (base bm25/fts vs fused), both gemini-3.1-pro answerer, temp 0 / seed 0, identical BaseAnswerer prompt. The load-bearing observation is that the end-to-end answer delta (fused−BM25 = −0.019) equals the recall delta to 3 decimals — no lever that fails on recall is rescued downstream, which licenses LLM-free recall@K as the proxy for the rest of the program. Reader confound is real and separable: on identical retrieval, gpt-5.4-nano scored 0.081/0.069 (abstains ~91%, 27/320 answered) vs gemini-3.1-pro 0.406/0.350 — ~5× — so e2e accuracy is reader-bound; gemini-3.1-pro is the headline anchor. (gemini-3.1-pro is a reasoning model: max_tokens:10 returned content:null; needs generous max_tokens.)

**Subset/tokenizer noise in the BM25-vs-FTS ordering (Experiment 3).** Ordering is subset-dependent: the §3 n=160 cut had BM25 ≥ FTS on every class, while a later R6 n=40 cut had FTS 0.80 > BM25 0.70 pooled. This flip is attributed to FTS5's tokenizer quality vs rank-bm25 and to small-N subset variance (per-class MDE ≈ 21pp @ n=160, ≈ 15pp @ n=40), not to the `b` parameter — all per-class deltas are direction-only at these N.

**Metric-definition math (shared scorer, `eval/p0a_base_retrieval.py` / `r2_parity_eval.py`).** `hit_at_k` returns None on abstention (empty gold), else 1.0/0.0; `multi_session` uses `gold_set.issubset(topk_set)`, all other classes use any-hit `any(g in topk)`; `reciprocal_rank` and `ndcg_at_k` use binary relevance with IDCG over min(|gold|,k); pooled = Σ(recall·n_scored)/Σ(n_scored) skipping None (n_scored-weighted, abstention-excluded). Class-balanced 2-pass subsample, fixed seed, full per-question haystacks never shrunk.

**RRF internals (Experiment 4).** RRF_K = 60.0 constant; unconditional vector⊕FTS5 with a vector-empty soft-fallback before collapse; no `fusion_mode` knob (HITL Option 1A, "documented-only/no-knob"). Determinism pinned by `pr_g9_rrf_fusion.rs` (6 green); filter=None and unfiltered paths proven byte-identical to 0.7.2, so additive features did not perturb the baseline (recall Δ = 0.0000). vec0 TEXT metadata is not NULL-able → empty-string status sentinel.

**Fused-path measurement validity caveats.** The fused arm is only meaningful with the doc vector kind registered (via the test-hooks `_configure_vector_kind_for_test('doc')` seam — no production Python surface registers a node vector kind) AND a coverage==1.0 embed gate: doc-only ingest with `use_embedder=True` is byte-identical to FTS-only unless the vector kind is registered. Coverage is verified by `verify_embed_db.py`, which joins `_fathomdb_vector_rows.kind` to live (`superseded_at IS NULL`) `canonical_nodes` per kind — this caught two real partial-embed DBs (a 0%-coverage DB where the doc kind was never registered, and a ~7% DB whose 1,408 vectors were all `edge_fact`, zero doc) that a naive `drain()`/projection-terminal count passed as complete. WAL detail: opens `mode=ro` (not `immutable=1`) so it sees committed-but-uncheckpointed rows as a live post-drain gate.

**Cross-encoder reranker internals + caveat (Experiment 5).** 0.8.1 R1 shipped the `rerank_fused` seam as a STUB (`try_get_loaded()→None`, `score()→0.0`); 0.8.2 E1 implemented a real TinyBERT-L-2 CPU CE (proven to promote the relevant passage in a Rust reorder test) and E2 added a standalone `fathomdb.rerank` API so the comparator reranks the *real* fused pool (codex [P1] fix). Built `--features fathomdb-engine/default-reranker`, footprint-preserving (no network at feature-off / rerank_depth=0). On valid data the CE is null: BAR F1 0.306 tied, M1 n=300 fused_rerank 0.415 < fused 0.450. The M1 F1 figures come from the multi-hop MuSiQue harness (gpt-5.4 reader, ≥3-hop cell), so they index multi-hop answer accuracy, not the LLM-free shallow-hop recall endpoint that defines this capability — included here only because the CE is a ranking-relevance lever.

**Known engine defect affecting shipped dense retrieval.** `CandleBgeEmbedder` defaults to `Pooling::Mean` for CLS-pooled bge-small (`candle_bge.rs:229`), which degrades shipped dense retrieval; the eval harness is unaffected because it uses CLS pooling. The 0.8.3 D1 CLS fix changes stored vectors and therefore must re-clear the eu7 ANN fidelity floor (≥0.90; the large-run value was 0.896 < floor, shipped via a one-sided CI gate) — i.e. fixing dense ranking is coupled to a fidelity-gate re-measurement (ADJ-3). The M1 underpoweredness and graph-arm negatives are out of scope for this capability but are the reason dense, not graph, is the carried-forward lever.

## Exploratory retrieval (graph-augmented & enrichment lift)

**Statistical confidence / power.** All three experiments are n=40 (10/class). Per-class MDE ≈ 15pp, so any single-class delta (e.g. multi_session 0.30→0.10) is direction-only, not significant in isolation. The load-bearing result that *is* robust is the graph **ON−OFF = 0.00** pooled delta, because it is a same-engine paired A/B with zero corpus/embedder variance: identical docs, identical index, identical kind=doc anti-pollution filter, only the BFS arm toggled. It reproduces both before and after the anti-pollution filter (pre-filter: graph_OFF = graph_ON = 0.60; the filter lifted both arms +0.05 equally to 0.65, leaving ON−OFF unchanged), confirming the IDF/length-norm distortion is upstream in corpus statistics, not fixable by a result-list filter. The prior base-retrieval context was n=160 (pooled MDE ~11pp, per-class ~21pp).

**Metric-definition math.** `hit_at_k` returns `None` for abstention (empty gold), else 1.0/0.0; `multi_session` uses `gold_set.issubset(topk_set)`, all other classes use `any(g in topk)`. Pooled recall = `Σ(recall·n_scored)/Σ(n_scored)`, skipping `None`. nDCG@10 is binary-relevance with IDCG over `min(|gold|, k)`. doc keys == gold answer_session_ids so hits score directly. All R6 cells had `abstention_total = 0`, `n_scored = 10/class`.

**Enrichment three-way decomposition (n=40).** bm25 0.70 → bm25_enriched 0.75 (**+0.05** content on tunable-b BM25); fts_only 0.80 → fts_enriched 0.775 (**−0.025** net on fixed-b FTS5); fts_placebo 0.70 (**−0.10** pure length penalty). Enriched−placebo = **+0.075** genuine content value; placebo isolates the length-norm cost. The placebo is rigorous: foreign single-token vocabulary, exactly length-matched to the real enrichment's added whitespace-token count, with the doc's *own* entity tokens excluded (foreign-only) and a process-stable blake2b-derived seed (codex §9 [P2] fixes — earlier salted built-in `hash()` was non-reproducible; multi-word names are split to single tokens so the length match is exact).

**Why the graph arm is a lower bound, not a wiring failure.** A 50/50 graph-arm probe resolved to real haystack sessions, so the arm is live. The C1 seeding strategy (commit 5da42b0) seeds the BFS frontier from the graph's *own* entity-node FTS (source B), **not** from the query's lexical top-K — exactly the configuration the IR literature says yields ~0 lift. Source-A (edge-fact FTS) seeding is OFF due to two engine bugs: an edge written *with a body* auto-enqueues an `edge_fact` vector projection (`lib.rs:8594`) that throws `SchedulerError` with no embedder configured, and a `StorageError` scale bug appears at ~8–14k edges. So the measured arm runs source-B + traversal only = a recall lower bound. Entity co-mingling hurt (−0.15) is BM25 document-length-normalization bias ("Verboseness Fission"), not result pollution — it persists upstream of the kind=doc filter.

**b-sweep internals.** 8 cells (4 b-values × {plain, enriched}) via `NaiveRAGAdapter`, with fixed-b `fathomdb_fts_only` as the bar. Monotone decrease of recall with increasing b confirms the length-norm mechanism at the parameter level. FTS5's b is not exposed, so the upside cell (enriched + low-b on FTS5's superior tokenizer) is structurally untestable without engine work; the FTS5-vs-rank-bm25 gap at matched b is attributed to tokenizer quality.

**Graph construction details.** Entities ingested as GLOBAL nodes (kind=entity, logical_id=`ent:<normalized-name>`, logical_id-alone identity per signed G0); per-session edges kind=rel referencing entity logical_ids; docs kind=doc. Extraction via Airlock vLLM batch (Qwen3.6-27B, enable_thinking=false, max_tokens=3072, 6000-char body cap, ~98% clean-parse with `_salvage_json` truncation recovery, $0 on 2× RTX-3090). Edges deliberately written body-less to dodge the projection bug above.

**Literature corroboration (adversarially verified 3-0).** Raw unseeded BFS adding ~0 over strong lexical is the expected result, not an anomaly: SPRIG (arXiv:2602.23372) HotpotQA BM25 R@10 0.742, GraphHybrid 0.775, plain RRF 0.851 — fusion beats the graph arm. The "graph beats BM25 ~46%" claim was refuted 0-3 (holds only with LLM-reasoning-guided traversal). BEIR-consistent: BM25 is a strong out-of-distribution baseline.

**Cross-capability corroboration (not part of this capability's registered endpoints).** The 0.8.2 M1 study re-adjudicated graph traversal fairly on *multi-hop answer accuracy* (MuSiQue, n=300, lexically-seeded Personalized PageRank — strictly dominates raw BFS — fused with BM25, reader gpt-5.4): ppr_fusion ΔF1 = −0.0405 vs the fused-RRF comparator, 95% paired-bootstrap CI [−0.116, +0.031] (n_boot=2000), CI upper bound below the +0.04 materiality threshold → robust NO-GO, reader-invariant across three readers. This corroborates the recall negative here: the graph lever fails on both the recall and answer-accuracy axes.

**Caveats.** (1) All deltas in this capability are n=40 directional except the ON−OFF null. (2) The graph arm is a lower bound (source-A seeding blocked by engine bugs), so a fairer upper-bound graph configuration was never measured at the LongMemEval recall layer — though M1's PPR upper-bound result (which dominates BFS) confirms the conclusion. (3) The single untested optimistic cell (fielded enrichment + tunable low-b on FTS5) is the only reason this capability is "non-win" rather than "refuted" — it requires custom-ranking engine work to evaluate.

## Answer synthesis (end-to-end generative QA)

**Why the −0.019 correspondence is load-bearing, not coincidence.** Experiment 1's end-to-end fused−BM25 accuracy delta (−0.019) equals the LLM-free recall@10 delta (R@10: fused 0.606, BM25 0.625, Δ −0.019) to three decimals. This 1:1 mapping is the formal justification for substituting cheap LLM-free recall@K for priced end-to-end runs across 0.8.1 — it demonstrates the reader neither amplifies nor masks retrieval differences on this corpus. Caveat: this is established at one N (160) on one corpus class-mix; it is a strong directional result, not a proven invariant.

**Statistical confidence (both experiments).** N=160, class-balanced (≈40/class). Per-class MDE ≈ 21pp; pooled MDE ≈ 11pp (seed 20260614, paired design). Therefore the −0.019 fused-vs-BM25 deltas (both recall and answer) are *well inside* the MDE — read as "tie/faithful pass-through," not a significant loss. Per-class orderings (e.g. KU fused 0.500 > BM25 0.475 > FTS 0.350; multi_session BM25 0.250 > fused 0.200 > FTS 0.150) are individually within per-class MDE and so are direction-only; their *consistency* across recall and answer axes is the credible signal, not any single cell. Note an ordering instability documented elsewhere: §3 n=160 had BM25 ≥ FTS, while a later R6 n=40 subset had FTS 0.80 > BM25 0.70 — attributed to tokenizer/implementation and subsample, not a real reversal.

**n_answered / abstention accounting.** Experiment 1 arms answered 89–95 of ~160 (the remainder includes legitimate abstain-correct cases and non-answers); accuracy is computed with abstention scored correct iff the gold is a negative/abstention case, and a non-abstaining wrong answer scored 0. Experiment 2's nano reader answered only 27/320 question-arm cells (~91% abstention) — this is *behavioral collapse*, not low accuracy on attempted answers, which is why nano is excluded as a headline reader rather than reported as a marginal arm.

**Judge vs substring.** Experiment 1's overall numbers (0.406 / 0.388 / 0.350) are reported under both a gemini-3.5-flash LLM judge and a normalized-substring `_match` proxy (the `normalize_answer` path maps "I don't know"/idk/empty → abstention via a shared `_ABSTAIN` set, identical in sync and batch). The two measures converge at the overall level on this run; no divergence metric between them was separately reported.

**Reader-swap mechanics / validity caveats.** gemini-3.1-pro is a reasoning model and required generous `max_tokens` (with `max_tokens:10` it returned `content:null`) — a measurement-validity trap that, if uncorrected, would have spuriously deflated the strong-reader arm. Both readers ran temp 0, seed 0, over a single shared `BaseAnswerer.PROMPT_TEMPLATE` (the "identical-answerer" invariant: adapters expose only `retrieve(question,k)`, so per-system prompt divergence is structurally impossible and the swap delta is attributable to the reader alone). Batch infra: OpenAI via litellm + AI-Studio Gemini via the aistudio batch gateway (~50% cost reduction); Vertex 3.x global offers no batch.

**Scope / what is *not* measured here.** These two experiments measure judged + substring *accuracy*, not answer-F1; answer-F1 is the registered endpoint of the separate multi-hop graph adjudications (0.8.2 M1, MuSiQue), which belong to a different capability and use a different reader (gpt-5.4) and a paired-bootstrap CI / power-gate / materiality-threshold decision rule not applicable to these recall-reader runs. No confident-wrong guard was separately evaluated for these LongMemEval runs. Data artifacts: `0.8.1-p0a-base-e2e-n160-gemini31pro.json`, `0.8.1-p0a-fused-e2e-n160-gemini31pro.json`, and (reader-swap) `p0a_base_n160_gemini31pro.json`.

## Retrieval fidelity & embedding integrity

**Fidelity gate — statistics and validity caveats.**
- Floor constant is **0.90, unchanged** (never lowered). The gate is `tests/support/recall_gate.rs::recall_ci_clears_floor(ci_hi, floor)` — a one-sided test that passes when the 95% CI upper bound >= floor and still fails for a CI lying entirely below floor. At N=7667 the measured point estimate was 0.8960 with CI [0.864, 0.925], so ci_hi = 0.925 >= 0.90 → PASS, point estimate notwithstanding.
- **Why 0.871 is not the fidelity number:** Slice 10 made `search()` an unconditional RRF hybrid (vector ⊕ FTS5), but the eu7 ground truth is vector-only exact-f32 top-10. Running eu7 on `search()` therefore scores fusion divergence (0.871), not quantization fidelity. A test-only vector-stage seam was required to recover the true fidelity number (0.896).
- **The 0.937 anchor was a conflation, not a measurement** on the current (byte-identical GA-1, N=7667) corpus. The honest read is 0.896 (0.8.0) vs 0.937 (0.7.x) on the same corpus = a genuine vector-path fidelity regression of ~4 points, motivating the bisect.
- **Vacuous-gate caveat (related, not the same gate):** `ac_013b` ASSERTS 0.90 but does so on the SYNTHETIC `VaryingEmbedder` (~0.73), so it was re-classified report-only; do not cite ac_013b as evidence the 0.90 real-embedder floor holds — it never measured it. The real-embedder fidelity number is eu7. (Several perf/recall ACs — ac_012/013/013b/019/020 — are AGENT_LONG-gated and thus never run in per-push CI.) eu7 perf optimizations (parallel GT re-embed + retained-f32 reuse) were deferred to 0.8.5.
- ANN config under test: 1-bit sign quantization, K=192 candidate fan-out, then f32 rerank against the exact-f32 top-10.

**Completeness verifier — metric definition and engine internals.**
- Coverage math: `coverage = n_docs_embedded / n_docs`, where `n_docs_embedded = COUNT(DISTINCT vr.write_cursor)` from `_fathomdb_vector_rows` JOINed to `canonical_nodes WHERE kind=? AND superseded_at IS NULL`. The JOIN to *live* nodes is load-bearing: it stops a stale/tombstoned cursor from inflating coverage, and per-kind attribution is what exposes the "vectors exist but they are all `edge_fact`, zero `doc`" failure mode.
- Six boolean checks (all must pass for `ok`): `vector_kind_registered`, `docs_present` (== `expected_docs` if supplied), `coverage_complete` (>= `min_coverage`, default 1.0), `vectors_present`, `dimension_correct` (== 384), `embedder_correct` (== `fathomdb-bge-small-en-v1.5`). `embedder`/`dimension` are read from `_fathomdb_embedder_profiles ORDER BY (profile='default') DESC LIMIT 1`.
- **Why naive checks false-pass:** a doc can be marked `_fathomdb_projection_terminal` *without* a vector being written — e.g. the vector kind was never registered, or a projection ran but aborted. `drain()` returning idle only proves the projection queue was processed, not that any vector landed. Both real failure DBs (`/tmp/r2-lme-s-nograph.sqlite`, 0% — `doc` kind never registered; `/tmp/r2-lme-s-v2.sqlite`, ~7% — 1,408 vectors all `edge_fact`) passed drain/terminal but were caught by attribution.
- **WAL trap:** the verifier opens `mode=ro` (NOT `immutable=1`). `mode=ro` sees committed-but-uncheckpointed rows in the `-wal` file, which is required for use as a live post-drain gate; `immutable=1` reads only the main DB file and would falsely fail every fresh build.
- `VerifyReport` carries `n_doc_vectors` (>= `n_docs_embedded` when chunked, since one doc can produce multiple vector rows). `assert_embed_complete` raises `EmbedIncompleteError(message, report)` listing the failed checks; the CLI exits non-zero unless `ok`. An optional `verify_queryable(retrieve, queries, k=10)` adds a functional complement (each probe must return >= 1 hit); the DB-level check itself is pure/read-only. Unit-tested in `tests/test_verify_embed_db.py`.
- Resumable-gate usage: `_drain_until_embedded` in `p0a_base_retrieval.py` polls drain in `chunk_s` slices, uses coverage as the completion oracle, stops on idle-but-incomplete (stuck) or stall (no progress for `stall_chunks`), and only serves fused recall at coverage == 1.0; resumable across process restarts.

## Engine performance (read/scan latency & concurrency)

**Reproducibility / stability of the dev-box numbers.** Multiple reruns confirm the winning arms are tight, not lucky single draws:
- W4.1-ac012: r1–r5 all land 40–41 / 95–97 ms (p50/p99).
- W4.1-stacked-O1: r1–r5 AC020 ×7.13–7.93.
- W4.0j: r3–r5 AC020 ×4.46–4.92 (the ×5.391 headline is the top of this band; the reruns sit slightly lower).

**Hardware confound — dev-box vs canonical CI (the load-bearing caveat).** The headline latencies are **dev-box only**. The few canonical-CI measurements (slower hardware) run materially higher, so the dev-box figures are optimistic and not yet budget-adjudicable:

| Arm | CI AC012 p50/p99 (ms) | CI AC020 speedup |
|---|---|---|
| W1.1 (baseline) | 162 / 526 | ×2.723 |
| W5.3 | 66 / 221 | ×3.074 |
| W4.1 stacked-O1 | 57 / 135 | ×3.143 |

W4.1-stacked-O1's concurrency speedup compresses from ~7–8× (dev-box) to **×3.143** on CI hardware — the absolute speedup ranking is preserved but the magnitude is hardware-sensitive, and the un-measured arms (W4.1-ac012, W4.0j, etc.) have **no** CI number at all. Any "3.2× read / 7–8× concurrency" claim is dev-box-conditioned.

**Verdict status.** Every AC012/AC013/AC020 verdict is PENDING — these are measurements against a lever sweep, not gated pass/fail outcomes. No budget threshold has been applied, so "winner" here means best-measured, not "meets AC".

**Measure definitions / corpus sizes.** AC012 = single-read p50/p99 at n=1,000,000; AC013 = scan p50/p99 at n=100,000; AC020 = seq vs conc wall-time with speedup = seq_ms / conc_ms. AC013 has a single measured arm (W4.1 L-D1-pcache2-O1, 2048/2327 ms), so there is no within-measure comparison for scan latency — treat scan as a single data point, not a sweep result.

**Lever taxonomy (the sweep dimensions).**
- L-A0 — baseline (no tuning).
- L-B1..B5 — pragma / page tuning.
- L-C1-k{10,100,1000} — LIMIT pushdown at varying k.
- L-D1-pcache2 — page-cache-2.
- L-Y1 — memstatus off (single largest concurrency lever; W4.0d memstatus-off alone already reached ×4.033 vs ~2.7× baseline).
- L-Y2 — page size 8k/16k.
- L-Y3-1GB — mmap sizing at 1 GB.
- O1 — stacking variant (top concurrency contributor when combined with Y1/Y2/Y3/B4/B5).

The concurrency mechanism is overhead-reduction: memstatus-off + larger pages + mmap lower per-operation CPU cost, freeing cores for parallel readers — hence the speedup ratio rises rather than single-thread latency alone.
