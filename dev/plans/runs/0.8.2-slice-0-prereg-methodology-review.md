## Verdict: NOT sound to freeze as-is — 4 load-bearing amendments + 2 advised.

The instrument's engineering is good (pre-reg frozen as imported code; strong-baseline stance;
unanswerable-set guard; NaN loud-fail; F1-primary; no-silent-caps). The flaw is **statistical**: the GO
gate biases toward the expected NO_GO, so it cannot credibly *earn* the index-key-enrichment pivot.

### Load-bearing (do before signing)
1. **Replace the strict monotonic `f1[2]<f1[3]<f1[4]` GO gate with a trend/contrast gate** (non-negative
   ΔF1-vs-hop slope; *negative*-trend veto only). Cited lit does NOT predict monotonic growth (HippoRAG:
   4-hop path-finding "out of reach" of single-pass PPR → expect flat or 3-hop-peaked); strict `<` on 3
   noisy estimates passes ~1/6 even under a true uniform +Δ. This is the dominant false-NO_GO source.
2. **Fix a single pre-registered comparator (fused-RRF), not a per-hop max** — the max-of-3 inflates the
   baseline (winner's curse) AND confounds the dose-response (different arm winning each hop).
3. **Material-lift gate = pooled ≥3-hop ΔF1 ≥ 0.02 with a paired-bootstrap CI lower bound > 0** (per-hop
   as secondary). Fix the design wording bug: threshold must be **at/above** the Slice-5 MDE, not "below".
4. **Add a whole-`decide()`-rule power simulation** at Slice 5 under ≥3 effect shapes (flat-positive,
   monotonic, inverted-U); gate `power_ok` on rule-level P(GO) ≥ 0.8 under flat-positive +0.03, not on a
   marginal per-hop MDE.

### Advised (not blocking)
5. **Soften the EM gate** to a CI-banded pooled ≥3-hop check; let the **unanswerable-set confident-answer
   rate** carry the confident-wrong role (binary EM noise can veto a real F1 win — a frozen test shows a
   single −0.01 EM killing a clean GO).
6. **Add a fused+cross-encoder-rerank baseline arm** (reuse the 0.8.1 R1 cross-encoder) and **pin RRF
   k=60** — otherwise a GO could be attributable to "we didn't rerank," a weak-baseline false GO.

### Keep (good)
Pre-reg-as-code; NaN loud-fail; F1-primary; unanswerable contrast set; strong-baseline-not-strawman; RRF
as fusion; same-answerer parity; no-silent-caps; body-less edges. Hop-stratification is valid (MuSiQue
k-hop = gold-passage count) — but make the **pooled ≥3-hop lift the PRIMARY** and per-hop a pre-registered
secondary feeding the trend gate.

Meta: every amendment moves the rule *less* conservative (more willing to call GO) — the correct direction
to scrutinize given the honest NO_GO prior.
