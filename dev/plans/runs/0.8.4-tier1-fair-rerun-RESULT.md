# 0.8.4 Tier-1 FAIR re-run — RESULT: the "decisive" GraphRAG loss was largely a measurement artifact

**Date:** 2026-06-23 · **Script:** `0.8.4-tier1-fair-rerun.py` · **Spend:** **$0.324** (gpt-5-nano both
sides; claude-haiku judge) · **Design:** `dev/design/0.8.4-closing-graphrag-gap.md` §2.

> **Headline.** Re-running the literal FathomDB-vs-Microsoft-GraphRAG head-to-head on the **same
> preserved index** with the three Tier-1 fairness levers — matched generation budget (600→1500
> tokens), raised k (8→15 = full coverage), MMR — **flips the result**: FathomDB's map-reduce arm
> goes from losing comprehensiveness 0.06 to **winning it 0.81** (and diversity 0.32→0.88). The
> earlier "comprehensiveness ≈ 0.00, decisive" was **mostly the 600-token reader cap + top-8**, not
> a structural inability to do sensemaking — **at this 15-doc scale.**

## Configuration (per HITL: use nano; keep FathomDB's gpt use equivalent to GraphRAG)

- **Shared answer/synthesis model = `gpt-5-nano` on BOTH sides** (FathomDB reader AND GraphRAG's
  global-search query LLM), identical `max_tokens=1500`. Procedurally fair on the synthesis axis.
- GraphRAG keeps its **gpt-5.4-built community reports** (115; its index investment). FathomDB Tier-1
  has **no index** — it reads raw text. Index asymmetry is **intentional** (Tier 2 is FathomDB's
  equivalent index; not built here).
- Judge `claude-haiku` (cross-family — an openai judge would break the self-preference control), 8
  AutoQ global questions, 5 runs, order-swapped, through `decide_084`. Same 15 AP-News docs both sides.

## Result (win-rate >0.5 = FathomDB better than GraphRAG)

| FathomDB arm | comprehensiveness | diversity | empowerment | decide_084 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| `fathomdb_mapreduce` | **0.812** [0.562, 1.000] | **0.875** [0.625, 1.000] | 0.425 [0.125, 0.725] | NOT_REACHED — **underpowered:comprehensiveness**; surpass=[comprehensiveness, diversity] |
| `fathomdb_vector` (k=15+MMR) | 0.525 [0.275, 0.775] | 0.669 [0.374, 0.919] | 0.312 [0.062, 0.625] | NOT_REACHED — underpowered:comprehensiveness |

vs the original (capped) run: mapreduce comp **0.062→0.812**, div 0.319→0.875; vector comp
**0.000→0.525**. The binding constraint is now **power (mde≈0.22 > ε=0.05 at n=8)**, NOT below-parity —
the map-reduce arm is measured **above** parity on 2 of 3 metrics.

## What this proves — and what it does NOT (read carefully)

**Proves:** the original decisive-looking loss was **dominated by two self-inflicted confounds** (the
`max_tokens=600` reader cap and the hardcoded top-8). Removing them, FathomDB's whole-corpus
map-reduce **matches or beats** Microsoft GraphRAG's global search **at 15-doc scale**. The length
fix is visible directly (FathomDB answers 3200c→~4700c, matching GraphRAG's ~6000c).

**Does NOT prove parity (four honest caveats):**
1. **Underpowered.** `decide_084 = NOT_REACHED` on power (n=8, mde≈0.22). This is a **diagnostic**,
   not a registered verdict. CIs are wide (some span to 1.0).
2. **Both sides on nano.** To honor "equivalent gpt use," GraphRAG's *synthesis* was also dropped from
   gpt-5.4 to nano — so GraphRAG is **not at its original synthesis strength** (it retains only its
   gpt-5.4 *index*). A skeptic can argue this weakened GraphRAG; a confirmation at **gpt-5.4-on-both**
   (~$2–4) removes that doubt.
3. **15-doc scale is GraphRAG's weakest case.** GraphRAG's community-summary advantage is designed to
   pay off at **large corpus**, where you *cannot* read everything and flat map-reduce gets lossy/
   expensive. At 15 docs, brute-force map-reduce *sees the whole corpus* → it competes by construction
   (the "long-context is almost enough" prior, `strong_baseline_clears`). **The real test of
   GraphRAG's structural edge is at 100s–1000s of docs** — exactly where FathomDB would need the Tier-2
   D2 coverage index.
4. **Vector-arm noise.** Two questions produced very short `fathomdb_vector` answers (106c, 828c —
   nano terseness), depressing that arm; the map-reduce arm is the robust signal.

## Implication for the program

- **The GraphRAG gap is NOT large at small scale; it was mostly the cap.** Fork C ("record the
  GraphRAG win") is **withdrawn** — recording the original 0.00 would have recorded an artifact.
- **The live question is now SCALE, not parity-at-15-docs.** Does fair FathomDB still match GraphRAG
  at 100s–1000s of docs, or does the structural gap reappear (where Tier-2 D2/D1 coverage summaries
  earn their keep)?
- **Recommended next:** (a) a **gpt-5.4-on-both confirmation** at 15 docs to kill the nano-weakening
  doubt (~$2–4), and/or (b) **scale up the corpus + power up** (the decisive experiment) — fair
  FathomDB (map-reduce + optional D2) vs GraphRAG at ≥100 docs, ≥~50 q, length-controlled, registered.
