## Result (directional) — and what the long_context window size does to it

`vector_rag` vs `long_context`, win-rate > 0.5 = vector_rag better (n=12 judgments,
36/36 headline cells decided). **Two runs, differing only in the `long_context` budget:**

| `long_context` budget | comprehensiveness | diversity | empowerment |
|---|---|---|---|
| 24k chars (~6 articles, undersized) | 1.000 | 1.000 | 1.000 |
| **120k chars (~30 articles, fairer)** | **0.833** [0.50,1.00] | **0.833** [0.50,1.00] | **0.833** [0.50,1.00] |

The single knob — how much corpus the long-context control gets to stuff — moved the
win-rate from 1.000 to 0.833 (CI lower bound now exactly at parity, 0.50). **At gpt-5.4's
far larger window (hundreds of articles, not 30), long_context would be stronger still** —
so the Samsung "VectorRAG is almost enough" prior is plausibly more competitive than even
0.833 implies. The registered run MUST size long_context to the answerer's real window.

## Methodological catch for the REGISTERED run (important)

The 1.000 sweep is partly an **artifact of an undersized `long_context` control**: at
24k chars it fits only ~6 of 1397 articles, **query-independently** (sorted by doc_id), so
for most questions the relevant article isn't present and the arm answers from irrelevant
context (observed: several long_context answers were 300–530 chars — the model had nothing
to say). That makes `long_context` a strawman in the WRONG direction (too weak), inflating
vector_rag's win.

**Fix before the registered run:** size the `long_context` budget to the **answerer's real
context window** (gpt-5.4 is large — fit as many articles as the window allows, minus answer
room), so it is the honest "stuff-it-all-in" upper bar the design intends. Otherwise the
premise check is meaningless. (The `LongContextAdapter` `char_budget` param already supports this.)

## Directional premise signal (caveated)

Even crude retrieval (BoW vector_rag) still beats a fairly-sized long-context (0.833) on this
corpus — i.e. **retrieval matters here**, consistent with the premise that a structured
retrieval/synthesis method (S1 community summaries) could add value. This is NOT the
"long_context saturates → weak premise" warning. **But the margin narrowed sharply (1.000→0.833)
when the control was sized fairly, and would narrow further at a real long-context window** — so
the premise is *live but not a slam-dunk*, and the result is underpowered (n=12, CI to 0.50) +
same-family-judged. Treat as "premise not refuted, proceed to a properly-sized registered pilot,"
not as evidence S1 will win.
