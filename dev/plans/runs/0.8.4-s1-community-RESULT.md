# 0.8.4 — actual community-based GraphRAG/S1 arm RESULT (the community structure HURTS)

**Date:** 2026-06-23 · **What:** an **actual community-based GraphRAG/S1 arm** — entity extraction
(Qwen) → article graph (edges = shared entities) → **real community detection** (pure-Python label
propagation; PEP 668 blocked pip) → **LLM community reports** (Qwen) → **global search** (map over
community reports → reduce, gpt-5.4 reader). Cross-family judged (claude-haiku, 5 runs, order-swapped),
`decide_084`. Built over a 60-article subset (24 communities). Script: `dev/plans/runs/0.8.4-s1-community.py`.
Spend ~$3–4 (gpt-5.4 global search + claude judge); running total this session ~$6.5.

## Headline: the community-summary structure LOSES — the win was the reader, not the graph

| arm (cross-family claude-haiku judge, gpt-5.4 reader) | vs long_context | vs vector_rag |
|---|---|---|
| **flat map-reduce over RAW text** (prior run) | **0.72 / 0.55 / 0.57 (WINS)** | **0.83 / 0.68 / 0.63 (WINS)** |
| **community-based S1** (this run; gpt-5.4 over community REPORTS) | **0.42 / 0.41 / 0.38 (LOSES)** | **0.39 / 0.47 / 0.39 (LOSES)** |

Same reader (gpt-5.4), same cross-family judge, same baselines — **the only change is what the reader
synthesizes over.** Mapping over **raw article text** wins; mapping over **community reports** (an offline
LLM-summarized index, the GraphRAG value-add) **loses.** `decide_084` = NOT_REACHED (underpowered).

## Interpretation (decision-grade, with the honest caveat)

- **The measured value came from the strong reader's synthesis over raw text — NOT from the GraphRAG
  community structure.** The community-summary indirection added a **lossy bottleneck** (the report
  compresses away detail the reader could otherwise use) and **hurt** — consistent with the program's
  cross-graph prior (M1/M2 graph negatives; the Samsung "VectorRAG is almost enough" result).
- **The load-bearing caveat:** the community reports were written by **weak local Qwen ($0)**. The whole
  GraphRAG bet is that GOOD community summaries add global coverage; cheap Qwen reports may simply be too
  lossy. A re-run with **gpt-5.4-written community reports** could change the verdict — this is the same
  "weak-synthesizer confound" that flipped the earlier pilot, now at the community-report layer.
- **Subset + simple communities:** 24 communities over 60 articles (sizes 9,9,6,5,4,3,3,2,2,2…) are
  fragmented — many tiny communities. Real GraphRAG over 1397 articles with Leiden would have richer,
  more meaningful communities. So this under-represents the real community quality too.

## What this adds to the resolution (the three data points)

1. **Flat map-reduce over raw text + gpt-5.4:** WINS (0.72–0.83) → strong-reader synthesis is the lever.
2. **Community-based S1 + Qwen reports + gpt-5.4 reader:** LOSES (0.38–0.47) → the cheap community-summary
   index is a lossy layer that *removes* value.
3. ⇒ **Provisional, caveated read:** the expensive GraphRAG community-summary machinery does NOT obviously
   pay off; the winning configuration is **gpt-5.4 map-reduce over raw retrieved/sampled text** — cheaper
   than the full S1 index. The graph/community structure has to *beat raw-text synthesis*, and with cheap
   reports it does not.

## The decisive, cheap next experiment (before funding the full S1 build)

**Re-run this community-based S1 with gpt-5.4-written community reports** (a few dollars). Two outcomes,
both decision-clean:
- If S1 with *strong* reports now beats the baselines → the community structure's value is real but
  requires strong-model summaries → fund the full S1 build (with a strong offline summarizer).
- If S1 with strong reports STILL loses to flat-map-reduce/baselines → the community-summary paradigm
  does not pay off for FathomDB on this corpus → **settle toward the publishable graph null**, and ship
  the cheaper "strong-reader map-reduce over raw text" instead. (A third graph null is a roadmap-sanctioned
  outcome.)

## Honest boundary (unchanged)

Still a SUBSET community build, label-propagation (not Leiden), Qwen reports, underpowered, and NOT
Microsoft's `graphrag` package nor a HippoRAG-2 multi-hop head-to-head. But it IS a real
community-detection GraphRAG mechanism — and its (caveated) finding that cheap community summaries *hurt*
is the sharpest signal yet on whether the expensive S1 build is worth funding.
