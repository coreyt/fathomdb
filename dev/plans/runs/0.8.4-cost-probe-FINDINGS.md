## The headline finding — the budget concern was over-stated

The Slice-0 §7 estimate ("plausibly $30–60+") assumed worst-case. **Measured, the realistic
cost is single-digit dollars per pair** with a Haiku/Sonnet judge — the **judge tier is the
dominant cost lever** (Opus-as-judge is ~4–5× Sonnet). For the full resolution, multiply the
**judge** leg by the number of pairs (the powered run adds `s1`-vs-`graphrag`,
`s1`-vs-`vector_rag`, `s1`-vs-`long_context`) and add the `s1`/`graphrag` answerer arms — so a
**full ~100-question multi-pair run lands at roughly ~$8–12 with a Haiku judge, ~$15–20 with
Sonnet** (Opus would blow it to ~$60–80). That is at/near the **~$11.84 remaining** of the $50
cap — so with a **Haiku/Sonnet judge a large top-up may not be needed**; an Opus judge is what
forces a top-up.

## Recommendation for the HITL spend gate

1. **Use a cheaper cross-family judge (Haiku or Sonnet), not Opus** — it is the dominant cost
   lever and BenchmarkQED-style pairwise judging does not need a frontier judge.
2. **Pin the chosen judge's real price** in `eval/gap_decomposition_run.py::PRICE_PER_1M`
   (currently Claude is unpinned → `price_for` fails closed — a required pre-run fix).
3. With a Haiku/Sonnet judge, the **premise pilot + the first powered pair fit the remaining
   budget**; approve a modest top-up only if standardizing on Sonnet/Opus or scaling questions.
