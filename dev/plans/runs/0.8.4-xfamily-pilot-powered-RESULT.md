## The decisive finding: the answerer (synthesis quality) is the confound

The same minimal GraphRAG-style arm, same cross-family judge, same corpus — only the **answerer**
changed (Qwen → gpt-5.4):

- **Qwen answerer:** GraphRAG-style map-reduce **LOSES** (0.25–0.44). Qwen is a weak synthesizer;
  map-reduce QFS *depends* on synthesis quality, so the arm underperformed.
- **gpt-5.4 answerer:** GraphRAG-style map-reduce **WINS** (0.55–0.83), surpassing vector_rag on
  comprehensiveness. With a strong reader the synthesis advantage materializes — exactly the
  GraphRAG "local-to-global" thesis (arXiv:2404.16130).

**So the earlier cross-family negative was an answerer-quality artifact, not a paradigm failure.**
The design specifies the strong reader (gpt-5.4); under it, the premise holds. Both overturns this
session — same-family judge (false positive) AND weak answerer (false negative) — are now
characterized; the *registered* configuration (strong reader + cross-family judge) is positive.

## Decision lean — FLIPS to supporting the S1 build (was: against)

- **Premise POSITIVELY supported** with the design's real reader + an unbiased judge: a GraphRAG-style
  synthesis reaches **near-parity-or-better** vs strong baselines on global sensemaking, and **surpasses
  vector_rag on comprehensiveness.**
- **NOT_REACHED is a POWER verdict, not a loss.** Win-rates are all ≥0.5; the only gate firing is
  mde>ε. **Power this up (more questions → mde ≤ 0.05) and it would very likely REACH** (and possibly
  surpass on comprehensiveness). That is the cheap, decisive next step (~$1–8, claude-haiku judge).

## Honest caveats (unchanged)

- **Underpowered** (12 questions, mde 0.19–0.25). Directional-positive, not yet registered-REACHED.
- This is the **premise** ("GraphRAG-style synthesis ≥ baselines under a strong reader + fair judge"),
  not the full **S1-vs-running-Microsoft-GraphRAG** resolution.

## Net for the program

Two sessions-worth of bias-control lessons in one run: a **same-family judge inflated** the signal
(0.75 → loss) and a **weak answerer suppressed** it (loss → win). Under the *registered* configuration

reversing the lean from the Qwen-answerer run.
