# 0.8.4 — GraphRAG community-summary paradigm: provisional RESOLUTION (the graph null, again)

**Date:** 2026-06-23 · **Status:** provisional resolution from a coherent set of cross-family-judged
pilots (underpowered; subset; not Microsoft's package — see "Honest boundary"). It is **decision-grade
for the fund/no-fund S1 gate**, not a fully-powered registered verdict.

## The question

Does a GraphRAG-style **community-summary** structure (the expensive S1 build) reach parity-or-better
vs strong baselines on global sensemaking, under an unbiased judge? (The 0.8.4 premise.)

## The evidence (all cross-family: claude-haiku judge, gpt-5.4 reader, ≥5 runs, order-swapped)

Win-rate vs the baselines (>0.5 = the GraphRAG-style arm is better; comprehensiveness shown, the others track):

| arm | vs `long_context` | vs `vector_rag` |
|---|---|---|
| **flat map-reduce over RAW text** | **0.72 (wins)** | **0.83 (wins)** |
| community-S1, **Qwen** reports | 0.42 (loses) | 0.39 (loses) |
| community-S1, **gpt-5.4** reports | 0.49 (~parity) | 0.32 (loses) |

Plus the two earlier control lessons:
- **Same-family judge inflates** (Qwen-judges-Qwen gave a false 0.75 "win"). Self-preference is real and large.
- **Reader quality is the dominant lever** (weak Qwen answerer made the *same* arm lose; gpt-5.4 made it win).

## The resolution (provisional, caveated)

1. **The GraphRAG community-summary structure does NOT pay off on this corpus — even with strong
   (gpt-5.4) community reports.** The community-S1 arm loses to plain `vector_rag` and is far below a flat
   strong-reader map-reduce over raw text. Strong reports helped (it reached ~parity vs `long_context`) but
   did **not** close the gap to `vector_rag` or to raw-text synthesis. The community report is a **lossy
   compression** whose information cost exceeds its global-coverage benefit here.
2. **The measured value is the STRONG READER synthesizing over RAW TEXT**, not the graph/community machinery.
   The winning configuration is `gpt-5.4 map-reduce / synthesis over raw retrieved text` — which FathomDB's
   existing retrieval already feeds. This is *cheaper* than the full S1 community-summary index.
3. **This is consistent with the program's whole cross-graph prior** (M1 multi-hop NO-GO; M2 dropped; the
   Samsung "VectorRAG is almost enough" result). It is the **third graph null** — a roadmap-sanctioned,
   publishable outcome: GraphRAG-style community summarization is **not** worth productizing in FathomDB on
   this evidence.

## Recommendation for the HITL fund/no-fund gate

**Do NOT fund the full S1 community-summary build on current evidence.** Instead:
- **Ship the cheaper winner:** strong-reader (BYO-LLM, caller-side) map-reduce / synthesis over FathomDB's
  raw retrieved text — it beat both baselines and the community structure.
- **If you want the registered verdict before settling:** the one cheap confirmation is to **power up the
  flat-map-reduce-over-raw-text vs baselines** comparison (more questions → `decide_084` mde ≤ 0.05) to log a
  REACHED/surpass for the *winning* config, and **power up the community-S1-with-strong-reports vs vector_rag**
  to log the null for the *community* config. Both are ~single-to-low-double-digit dollars on claude-haiku.

## Honest boundary (do not over-read)

- **Underpowered:** ~10–12 questions per comparison (`decide_084` = NOT_REACHED on power, mde 0.18–0.31).
  Directions are consistent across runs but not registered-REACHED.
- **Subset + simple communities:** 60-article build, label-propagation (not Leiden), 24 fragmented
  communities. A full-corpus Microsoft GraphRAG with hierarchical Leiden communities (C0–C3) *could*
  differ — but the mechanism (community summary as a lossy index) showed a consistent negative across two
  report-quality levels, which is the informative signal.
- **Not the literal Microsoft GraphRAG / HippoRAG-2 packages.** Standing those up (deps blocked by PEP 668;
  embeddings endpoint; multi-day indexing) remains the path to a *fully literal* head-to-head — but the
  paradigm-level read here (community summaries don't beat raw-text synthesis) is what funds-or-kills the build.
