## Read (caveated)

- **Direction is positive and matches the GraphRAG literature:** the map-reduce sensemaking
  arm beats *both* vector-RAG and long-context on global questions (0.750 each). GraphRAG's
  reported sensemaking advantage over vector-RAG reproduces *directionally* here.
- **Mechanism observation:** on the question where retrieval/window had little relevant
  material (vec=462c, lon=353c answers), the map-reduce arm still produced a substantive
  ~2944c answer — its broad scan surfaces global material a top-k retrieve or a window-fit
  stuff misses. That is exactly the sensemaking edge the design hypothesizes.
- **NOT trustworthy as a verdict:** underpowered (n=8, CI [0.25,1.00] spans parity), same-family
  judged, 40-article subset (under-covers vs a full GraphRAG index, if anything *understating*
  the global-coverage edge). 0.750 ≠ a parity/surpass claim.

## Why this matters for the program

This is the **premise validation the pilot-first gate wanted**, now *positive* rather than
merely "not refuted": a GraphRAG-style synthesis mechanism directionally beats strong-ish
baselines on the sensemaking axis on this corpus. Combined with the earlier vector_rag-0.833-
vs-long_context read, the **sensemaking premise for S1 is live and directionally supported** —
a reasonable basis to fund the minimal S1 build (Slice 10), subject to the HITL.

The map-reduce reader built here is a working **minimal S1 QFS reader** (the Slice-15 keystone
mechanism), proven to execute end-to-end on a live endpoint and to directionally win.
