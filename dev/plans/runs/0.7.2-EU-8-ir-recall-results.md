## Synthesis (in the IR-vs-ANN framework)

| Signal | Value | Tests | Reading |
|---|---|---|---|
| ANN recall | **0.937** | index / sign-bit quantization fidelity (GT = exact f32 KNN, same model) | healthy; clears 0.90 floor |
| IR recall | **0.571** | embedder semantic relevance (GT = labeled relevant doc ids) | the ceiling; relation-dependent |

**ANN (0.937) exceeds IR (0.571) by +37pp.** By the standard rule (once ANN fidelity
exceeds the embedder's IR ceiling by ~15-20%, further index/quantization tuning yields
no perceptible user gain), the verdict is unambiguous:

- **The quantization is NOT the bottleneck.** It faithfully returns ~94% of the exact
  neighbors. Chasing ANN recall higher (bumping K / `TOP_K_BIT_CANDIDATES`, tighter
  index) is wasted compute.
- **The embedder/task is the ceiling.** The levers that move end-to-end quality are a
  better embedder (semantic) or **using the graph** for relation-following -- NOT the
  vector pipeline.

### Important caveats on the IR number
1. **Synthetic relationship task, not human qrels.** Chains are generated by
   `tests/corpus/scripts/generate_chain_corpus.py`. Some relations are *inherently
   wrong for pure vector search*: **contradicts** (0.29) -- a contradicting doc is not
   reliably *near* in embedding space; cosine cannot capture logical negation. Those
   relations are precisely what the engine's **graph edges** exist for, and EU-8 search
   deliberately does not traverse them. So the 0.571 understates pure-semantic
   retrieval: the similarity-friendly relations (action_from / follows_up_on /
   summarizes) average ~0.66.
2. **Duplicate-body mapping.** 428 dup bodies; the body->doc_id first-occurrence rule
   can map a content-correct retrieval to a different id than expected, modestly
   undercounting. Clean fix (same lesson as the ANN harness): have the engine search
   return doc ids, not just bodies.
3. Dev-box scouting (synthetic queries). Canonical relevance eval remains future work.
