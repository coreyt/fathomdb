## Verdict: CONCERN

### 1. [medium] Panic-class parity is only enforced on the async engine paths, not every `#[napi]` entry point

Refs:

- `src/rust/crates/fathomdb-napi/src/lib.rs:16`
- `src/rust/crates/fathomdb-napi/src/lib.rs:268`
- `src/rust/crates/fathomdb-napi/src/lib.rs:432`

The module-level contract says every `#[napi]` entry point catches
Rust panics and rethrows the documented `FDB_PANIC` /
`FathomDbPanicError` class. That is true for the async engine calls
routed through `call_engine()` and for `Engine.open()`, but the
synchronous exported methods `counters()`, `set_profiling()`,
`set_slow_threshold_ms()`, and `attach_subscriber()` are plain
`#[napi]` methods with no explicit `catch_unwind` wrapper.

That means a panic escaping one of those entry points would fall
back to napi-rs's default panic conversion instead of the documented
typed panic path. This is a contract gap rather than a demonstrated
happy-path failure, but it means the Phase 11b panic guarantee is
not uniformly enforced at the FFI boundary.
