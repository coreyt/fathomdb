# PR-2a run report -- mean-centering recall investigation (experiment)

**Slice**: 0.7.2 PR-2a (experiment; measurement only -- no engine/ADR/floor/K/pin
change). **Date**: 2026-05-30. **Branch**: main. **Path**: offline Python over
EU-0's saved bge-small vectors (no re-embed of the corpus; ~100 query texts
re-embedded for the query-set sensitivity run).

**One-line verdict**: **GO** on a mean-representativeness fix. The production
first-256 ingest-order mean is pinned on a single source and is the dominant
driver of EU-7's depressed recall (-10.9pp under EU-7's own query methodology);
a representative 256-doc sample mean recovers recall to the full-corpus upper
bound. PR-2 should NOT lock ~0.79.

---

## 1. Setup and what is held fixed

Everything except the **mean vector** (and, in the sensitivity run, the **query
set**) is held to EU-0's validated offline pipeline:

- model bge-small (BAAI/bge-small-en-v1.5), dim 384, mean-pool + L2-norm
- K = 192 (ADR), sign-bit quant, f32 cosine rerank, top-10
- ground truth = brute f32 cosine over the full N=7667 corpus, target excluded
- recall@10 = |retrieved ∩ gt| / 10; percentile bootstrap, 1000 resamples, SEED=0xE0
- N = 7667 (the full real corpus), 100 queries

The mean strategy changes ONLY the vector subtracted before sign-bit
quantization. Ground truth and the f32 rerank are mean-invariant, so each
strategy isolates the mean's effect on quantization quality exactly as
production experiences it.

## 2. Gap-attribution table

### 2a. Mean strategies, EU-0 query methodology (quota-spread across all 8 sources)

| strategy | recall@10 | 95% CI | sigma | what it isolates |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **M0** first-256 ingest (all bahmutov) | 0.886 | 0.852..0.916 | 0.0165 | production pin today |
| **M1** full-corpus mean | **0.933** | 0.912..0.953 | 0.0106 | EU-0 behavior; reproduces 0.933 exactly |
| **M2a** stratified-256 (mean of 3 seeds) | 0.933 | range 0.931..0.935 | ~0.011 | the production-feasible fix |
| **M2b** uniform shuffled-256 (3 seeds) | 0.932 | range 0.926..0.943 | ~0.011 | simpler fix variant |
| **M3** no centering | 0.910 | 0.879..0.936 | 0.0149 | value of centering at all |

M1 = 0.933 reproduces EU-0 to the digit -> the **offline pipeline is validated**.

### 2b. Mean strategies, EU-7 query methodology (pool = first-1000 docs; the faithful 0.828 reproduction)

| strategy | recall@10 | 95% CI | sigma | cos(mean, full) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **M0** first-256 ingest | **0.842** | 0.804..0.885 | 0.0205 | **0.821** |
| **M2a** stratified-256 (3 seeds) | 0.945..0.951 | ~0.919..0.973 | ~0.013 | 0.998 |
| **M2b** uniform shuffled-256 (3 seeds) | 0.945..0.952 | ~0.918..0.975 | ~0.013 | 0.997 |
| **M1** full-corpus mean | 0.951 | 0.924..0.974 | 0.0127 | 1.000 |
| **M3** no centering | 0.918 | 0.891..0.944 | 0.0135 | -- |

EU-7's real engine measured **0.828**. Offline **M0 + EU-7-style queries = 0.842**
-- within **1.4pp** and inside EU-7's CI (0.796..0.858). Once the query
methodology matches, the offline pipeline reproduces the real production number.
This is the single strongest validation in the study and does not depend on any
additivity assumption.

### 2c. Attribution of the 0.933 -> 0.828 (10.5pp) total gap

| factor | move | delta | role |
|---|---|---|---|
| Query methodology | M1: EU-0 q 0.933 -> EU-7 q 0.951 | **+1.8pp** | EU-7 queries are marginally *easier* with a good mean -- does NOT explain the drop; it masks it |
| **Mean strategy** | M1 0.951 -> M0 0.842 (EU-7 queries) | **-10.9pp** | **the dominant driver** |
| Real-engine path | offline M0 0.842 -> real 0.828 | -1.4pp | candle-vs-HF embed + exact query set; within CI |

Net: 0.951 - 0.109 - 0.014 = 0.828. The mean is essentially the entire story.

**Truncation**: EU-7 uses 512-token truncation (`CandleBgeEmbedder`,
`eu7_real_corpus_ac.rs:99`), identical to EU-0's `max_len=512`. Truncation is
**matched** between the two anchors and therefore contributes **0pp** to the
EU-0<->EU-7 gap. (Truncation stays ON in production regardless; a within-corpus
long-doc sensitivity run was not needed to attribute the gap.)

**Headline secondary finding**: M0 (0.842 / 0.886) is *worse* than M3 no-centering
(0.918 / 0.910). Centering on a non-representative single-source mean is **actively
harmful** -- worse than not centering at all. A representative mean flips it to a
net gain. The `cos(mean, full)` column shows why: the bad mean is only 0.82
aligned with the true corpus mean; any 256-doc representative sample reaches 0.998.

## 3. Verdict -- GO / NO-GO

**Pre-registered threshold (fixed before seeing M2):** GO iff M2 (representative
256-sample) >= 0.88 AND M2 within ~0.02 of M1.

- M2 = **0.945..0.951** (EU-7 queries) / 0.932..0.933 (EU-0 queries) -- clears 0.88 by a wide margin.
- |M2 - M1| = 0.003 (EU-7 q) / 0.000 (EU-0 q) -- within 0.02.

**VERDICT: GO.** Both criteria met decisively under both query methodologies.
A representative 256-doc sample mean is statistically indistinguishable from the
full-corpus mean (cos 0.998; recall within 0.3pp) -- so the fix does **not** need
the whole corpus, just a representative sample at pin time.

**Recommended next step**: spawn **PR-2b** -- change the production pin to compute
`mean_vec` over a *representative* sample (shuffle/stratify the candidate set
rather than taking ingest-order first-256), then RED->GREEN + re-measure on the
real engine before PR-2.

## 4. Recommended number for PR-2

**Do NOT lock the floor at ~0.79.** That number is an artifact of a fixable
single-source mean pin, not an inherent embedder limit.

- If PR-2b ships: offline projects recovered recall@10 ~ **0.945** under EU-7's
  faithful methodology (M2 mean), sigma ~ 0.013, CI ~ 0.919..0.969; lower bound
  R - 2sigma ~ **0.92** -- back at/above the original 0.90 floor.
- **PR-2 must anchor its floor on the RE-MEASURED real-engine number after PR-2b**
  (and ultimately PR-3's canonical N=1M), not on this offline projection. Offline
  says "expect the floor back near/above 0.90"; real-engine + canonical confirm it.
- If for any reason PR-2b is declined, the honest accepted number is EU-7's
  **0.828** (R - 2sigma ~ 0.795, round-down 0.79) -- but with this evidence on
  record that it is depressed by a config artifact, not a model ceiling.

## 5. Residual risks (what offline cannot capture)

1. **candle-vs-HF embedding path (~1.4pp)**: offline uses HF transformers;
   production uses `CandleBgeEmbedder`. Bounded small -- offline reproduces the
   real EU-7 number within its CI. Still PR-2b's job to re-measure through the
   engine.
2. **Dev-box scouting, 100 queries, N=7667**: not the canonical verdict. PR-3's
   canonical N=1M with larger / relevance-judged held-out queries remains the
   verdict-quality signal.
3. **Topic-drift-after-pin**: a representative *initial* sample fixes the
   single-source-ingest artifact but does NOT protect a workspace that pivots
   topics after the 256-doc pin. See the re-determine-mean trigger analysis
   (Section 6) -- the data does not justify adding a recompute trigger now;
   reindex remains the documented topic-drift remedy.
4. **Attribution additivity** is approximate; the faithful EU-7-query
   reproduction (M0 = 0.842 ≈ real 0.828) is the load-bearing result and does not
   rely on it.

## 6. Should we set/leverage a "re-determine mean" trigger during ingestion?

(Question raised by HITL during this slice. Analysis only -- no engine change.)

**Two distinct mechanisms must not be conflated:**

1. **Representative *initial* pin** -- *what* the once-only mean is computed over
   at the 256-doc threshold (ingest-order first-256 vs a shuffled/stratified
   256-sample). This is PR-2b.
2. **Re-determine / recompute *trigger*** -- recomputing the mean later during
   ingestion (row-count milestones, drift telemetry), re-quantizing all sign
   bits each time. This is the heavier mechanism rejected for 0.7.1
   (`embedder-decision.md` §3.4) as scope creep, with reindex as the remedy.

**What PR-2a's data says about the trigger's importance:**

- The entire measurable loss comes from the *initial* pin being
  non-representative (single source). Once the initial sample is representative,
  **M2 (256-sample) = 0.945 ≈ M1 (full 7667-corpus) = 0.951** and `cos(mean,
  full) = 0.998`. A representative 256-sample mean is statistically
  indistinguishable from the full-corpus mean.
- Therefore the marginal gain of a *recompute trigger* over a representative
  *initial pin* is ~0.3-0.6pp -- inside the noise. **Recomputing later buys
  essentially nothing** once the first pin is representative, because 256
  representative docs already estimate the corpus mean as well as all 7667.
- The one scenario where a recompute trigger genuinely helps is **true topic
  drift** (workspace pivots topics *after* the pin). PR-2a's fixed multi-source
  corpus does **not** model that, so the experiment **cannot** measure a
  topic-drift benefit and provides **no evidence** for the trigger's value there.

**Recommendation: do NOT add a re-determine-mean trigger now.**

- The representative-initial-pin fix (PR-2b) is simpler, captures essentially
  all the benefit PR-2a can measure (+10.9pp), and needs no ingestion-time
  re-determination, no background recompute job, and no full re-quantize.
- A recompute trigger adds real cost (background mutation infra + sign-bit cache
  invalidation + drift telemetry) for a benefit this study cannot demonstrate.
  Keep it deferred; reindex stays the documented topic-drift remedy
  (`embedder-decision.md` §3.4).
- If drift safety is wanted later, it deserves its own telemetry-backed design
  (distribution-shift signal, not row-count) -- not justified by PR-2a's numbers
  and out of scope for the 0.7.2 release-hardening campaign.

So: **leverage representativeness at the initial pin (PR-2b), not a recompute
trigger during ingestion.**
