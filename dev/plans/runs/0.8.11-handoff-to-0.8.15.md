> **READ FIRST — load-bearing caveat.** Everything 0.8.11 produced (the per-intent config tuples,
> the classifier accuracy, the mis-route matrix, the VoI thresholds, the EXP-AF KILL, the
> arm-switching read) is **provisional SCREENING data, not validated config.** It was run to
> *de-risk and direct* the 0.8.15 dispatcher build — **not** to settle lasting verdicts. **No 0.8.15
> (or later) build may consume these outputs as a contract until the Pre-0.8.15 Validation Gate (§2)
> passes.** This doc travels with the tuples; the per-intent registry (`dev/prototypes/l2-router/registry.json`),
> the experiments-ledger 0.8.11 section, and `STATUS-0.8.11.md` all point here.
>
> **The one-line throughline (HITL 2026-06-28):** the embedder ceiling (~0.571 IR relevance),
> arm-switching ≈0, and the EXP-AF "recall-bound" finding all say the **binding constraint is
> recall/substrate — not routing-cleverness or agent-feedback.** Improve the recall functions
> *first*, then re-run the router/agent experiments on the better substrate.

## 3. Corrected findings — do NOT over-read these

- **Arm-switching is NOT "irrelevant."** The measured claim is: *static per-class* arm selection
  buys ≈0 **on LME** because **fused-RRF already absorbs BM25+vector** (it's the strong floor that
  beat the graph arm). It is **not** "arm choice never matters." The **per-query** oracle is
  unmeasured (V-2), and a lexical/entity-heavy corpus (BEIR Touché/NFCorpus) could read very
  differently. Treat as "RRF dominates the arms on LME," nothing stronger.
- **EXP-AF KILL is current-substrate-provisional, not a permanent verdict.** Its own mechanism —
  the agent realized only ~6% of headroom because the answer often isn't in the pool (recall-bound)
  — means **improving recall (wider candidate-gen, better embedder, FTS≥BM25) could flip it.** And
  it never tested **multi_hop**, the class where iterative agent reasoning is most theorized to pay.
  The 0.8.11-scope decision it *does* support: don't wire an unproven feedback arm into a screening
  prototype, keep `record_feedback` instrumentation. Re-test under V-3. **Do not conclude
  "agent-feedback is dead."**
- **The embedder is the ceiling.** ~0.571 IR relevance caps the vector path and much of routing's
  upside; ANN fidelity (0.937) ≫ relevance, so ANN/K tuning buys ≈0. The highest-impact (and
  hardest, separately-gated) lever is a stronger cross-vendor embedder — not router cleverness.

## 7. Competitor head-to-head (for V-6) — landed

Three committed reports back this section (now tracked despite the `dev/research/` gitignore):
**`planner-router-survey.md`** (34 approaches × 6 families; per-approach matrix + "tested-against"),
**`planner-router-secondary-review.md`** (FathomDB positioned vs the field — the §2a recommendations
above are its distillation), and **`planner-router-competitors.md`** (the original shortlist).
De-facto standard baselines: Self-RAG, FLARE, IRCoT, Self-Ask, Adaptive-RAG; standard multi-hop
triple HotpotQA + 2WikiMultiHop + MuSiQue. Differentiator confirmed-but-narrow: **no surveyed system
routes over per-intent operator config/knobs** (where our measured win lands). Gap: 3 of 5 intents
(multi_session/temporal/global) have no routing-lit analogue → apples-to-apples only on needle + multi_hop.

Full report: `dev/research/planner-router-competitors.md`. Shortlist: **Adaptive-RAG** (NAACL 2024,
arXiv:2403.14403 — closest match: query-complexity classifier → no/single/iterative retrieval, and
**reports a "w/ Oracle" ceiling = the direct analogue of our Gate-2 oracle**), Self-RAG, FLARE,
DRAGIN, Self-Route (RAG-vs-long-context), **RouterRetriever** (routes expert embedders, **reports on
BEIR**), and the OSS RouterQueryEngine/LangChain selectors. Multi-hop baselines for our `multi_hop`
class: **IRCoT, HippoRAG/HippoRAG-2, ReAct-retrieval** (all on MuSiQue/2Wiki/HotpotQA). Note: there
was **no standard router benchmark** until **RAGRouter-Bench** (arXiv:2602.00296, Jan 2026 —
availability unverified).

**Recommended head-to-head (V-6 primary):** **Adaptive-RAG vs FathomDB on MuSiQue**, metrics
EM/F1 + router accuracy + steps-per-query (cost), reported alongside the **oracle-vs-learned gap**
(their own vocabulary — frames our Gate-2 ceiling natively). Both code + corpus on disk; effort
**medium** (reproduce Adaptive-RAG's baseline *locally* — do not quote its hosted-LLM paper numbers —
to stay fair to our CPU-default/optional-LLM posture).

**Cheaper secondary (settles the arm-switching thesis directly):** **RouterRetriever-style
arm-routing on BEIR** (FiQA/Touché-2020/NFCorpus/ArguAna), nDCG@10 / Recall@k — pure-retrieval,
CPU-only, low effort; directly tests our internal "static arm-selection ≈ RRF" claim against their
"+2.1 nDCG@10 from routing" — and pairs with **V-2** (the per-query arm oracle).

**Honest caveat:** competitors route on retrieve-or-not / which-LLM / which-embedder; FathomDB routes
on per-intent retrieval *config*. Head-to-heads compare **end-task quality + cost**, not mechanism
parity. Our distinctive `multi_session`/`temporal`/`global` classes have **no** routing-literature
benchmark (they live in LongMemEval/LOCOMO/AP-News) — a gap to acknowledge, not paper over.
