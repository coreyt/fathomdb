## 2. The recall challenge — FULL background (so you can drive the ruling well)

This is the crux. Internalize the whole arc before you frame anything to HITL.

### 2.1 Where the 0.90 floor + the 0.937 anchor came from
- The recall floor is **≥ 0.90 recall@10 vs f32 brute-force ground truth**, HITL-locked in
  `ADR-0.7.0-vector-binary-quant.md` § 2 (the binary-quantization ADR — bit-KNN + f32 rerank must preserve the
  exact-f32 top-10). It is a **product-quality** floor.
- In **0.7.2 PR-2** the recall number was **reframed**: an earlier **0.828 reading was a measurement
  artifact** (exclude-after + body-string ground truth); the corrected **ANN-fidelity recall@10 = 0.937
  (CI 0.913–0.957)**, and the **floor was kept at 0.90**. ([[pr2a-go-recompute-split]].) **That 0.937 was
  measured on the THEN-current corpus (pre-expansion).** This matters: the anchor is corpus-specific.

### 2.2 How the gate was *wrong* before Slice 40 (the conflation)
- Across the whole 0.8.0 campaign the "recall floor holds (~0.937)" claim was a **conflation**
  ([[perf-recall-gates-masked-and-ac013b-conflation]]): the test that *asserted* 0.90
  (`perf_gates::ac_013b_recall_at_10_floor`) ran a **synthetic isotropic `VaryingEmbedder`** (a noise-limited
  fidelity number ~0.73–0.89, which **can't and shouldn't** clear a 0.90 *product* floor), while the **real
  bge-small** measurement (`eu7_real_corpus_ac.rs`) that actually hit 0.937 was **report-only** (it asserted
  only a loose "beats the noise floor" bound). Worse, all the heavy gates are `AGENT_LONG`-gated and run in
  **no per-push CI** — so the floor assert had effectively **never run**.
- **Slice 6** confirmed the sibling pattern (the project had already done the analogous fix for latency:
  **AC-072/AC-073** made `eu7` the *asserting verdict* and demoted synthetic `perf_gates` to report-only).

### 2.3 What Slice 40 did (the gate restructure — landed-correct on the branch)
- **AC-075 minted:** `eu7_real_corpus_ac.rs` now **hard-asserts real-embedder recall@10 ≥ 0.90**;
  `perf_gates::ac_013b_recall_at_10_floor` **demoted to report-only** (synthetic fidelity, informational); the
  fast sentinel `ac_013b_floor_matches_adr` still pins the 0.90 constant to the ADR; `ADR-0.7.0-vector-binary-
  quant.md` § 2 amended (floor gated on the real-embedder eu7). **This is the correct gate** — it measures the
  real product, finally.
- **Measurement model (mirrors AC-072/073, because the real embedder is slow):** the eu7 verdict is a
  **LOCAL once-per-release / `perf-canonical` (workflow_dispatch, `--release`)** exercise — **NOT per-push CI**
  (real-embedder canonical N is infeasible per-push; [[pr3-tiered-latency-budget]]). Per-push keeps only the
  read-path smoke canary.

### 2.4 The new finding: the corpus moved under the floor
- Running the **now-asserting** eu7 verdict (`--release`, isolated, real bge-small, **method `EU7_RECALL_NEW`
  = exclude-before + dedup-by-id GT, K=192, 100 queries, bootstrap 1000**, ~108 min wall):
  - **N=1,000 → recall@10 = 0.9100** (CI 0.882–0.935, σ 0.0136) — **PASSES.**
  - **N=7,667 → recall@10 = 0.8710** (CI 0.835–0.904, σ 0.018) — **FAILS the 0.90 floor.**
- **Root cause: the corpus grew out-of-band.** The 0.937 anchor was 0.7.2 on the **pre-expansion** corpus. The
  corpus is now **8 datasets / 7,667 docs** in `data/corpus-data/raw/`
  (`bahmutov_dailylogs`, `chain_connectives`, `cnn_dailymail`, `enron`, `enronqa`, `landes_todos`, `qmsum`,
  `synthetic_notes`) — an **owner-managed expansion integrated around the Slice-5 push (post-0.7.2)** (the
  former `corpus-work` worktree). So a **bigger, harder, more diverse corpus** lowered ANN-fidelity recall
  below 0.90. **The gate is correct; the product (on this corpus) does not clear it.**
- **DO NOT lower the floor or weaken the assert.** The whole point of the restructure was an honest gate;
  defeating it would re-create the vacuous-green trap.
