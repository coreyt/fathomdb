### 3.1 ADR (1) — `ADR-0.8.0-confidence-vs-importance` (F9 vs G12)
- **[shape]** Disentangle epistemic **confidence** (evidence-driven: strengthens with corroboration/source-
  count, decays with age-unconfirmed, drops on contradiction — Zep/Graphiti) from **G12 importance**
  (salience). Concretely define both.
- **[empirical] one signal or two for 0.8.x?** Decide the *default* (recommend the lower-commitment option,
  e.g. importance-only / single-signal, as the 0.8.x baseline) AND name the **consumer-validation
  experiment** (which consumer workloads, what metric distinguishes "two signals materially help" from "one
  suffices", the decision-rule for adding the second signal). Do **not** assert two-signals-needed without it.
- **[shape]** Storage locus: vec0/projection `REAL` with the 3-way sentinel reshape (same as G12 importance);
  **edges-resident confidence + `t_valid`/`t_invalid` = RESERVED** (graph-model H3) — decide nodes-now,
  reserve edges. Population policy options (writer-supplied vs corroboration-derived vs LLM-scored app-side)
  — recommend the 0.8.x-reachable one, reserve the rest. Decay model: state the shape; gate any tuning const
  on the experiment.
- **[shape]** Anchor the confidence-update contract to **G0 invalidate-not-delete** (Slice 15). Confirm any
  reweight happens **AFTER bit-KNN** (never a vec0 predicate), behind a **dedicated recency/importance flag —
  NOT `fusion_mode`**; surfaces optionally in the G1 hit payload (Py+TS lockstep); **no new read verb**.

### 3.2 ADR (2) — `ADR-0.8.0-fielded-fts-bm25f` (F5 / G9)

  weights** (smallest blast radius). **REJECT** (EXCLUDE list) the v0.5.x per-kind `fts_props_<kind>` table

- **[empirical] go/no-go + the column weights.** Baseline the CURRENT single-body FTS5 retrieval quality on
