## Brief history (how we got here — read once)

1. **EU-7** measured real-corpus recall@10 = **0.828**, below the locked 0.90 floor.
2. **PR-2a** (offline investigation) attributed the gap to a *mean-centering artifact*
   (the corpus mean is pinned on the first-256 ingest-order docs, which are a single
   source) and returned **GO** on a "fix the mean" path, projecting recovery to ~0.945.
3. HITL split the fix into **PR-2b** (production mean *recompute*: drift detector +
   `doctor recompute-mean` verb) and **PR-2c** (representative *initial* pin: reservoir
   + deferred settle=2048). Both were **built, GREEN, and ratified** (drift policy
   0.95 / debounce 256 / EWMA 1/256).
4. **Real-engine re-measure + root-cause investigation overturned PR-2a's premise:**
   - Forcing the *full-corpus* mean on the real engine gives **0.847** -- the best
     possible mean is only **+1.9pp** over the worst. **The mean is a non-lever.**
   - candle vs HF embeddings are **bit-identical** (not the cause).
   - The offline->real gap was **~6pp of conservative harness measurement methodology**
     (target excluded *after* top-10; body-string GT with 5.6% duplicate bodies), NOT
     the engine/mean/embeddings.
   - **Corrected ANN recall@10 = 0.937** (CI 0.913-0.957) -- *clears the 0.90 floor.*
   - **IR recall@10 = 0.571** (embedder semantic ceiling) << ANN fidelity 0.937 -> the
     quantization is not the bottleneck; the embedder/task is.

   So: **recall was never broken.** PR-2a's GO rested on an offline number that does not
   transfer to the production retrieval+measurement path.
