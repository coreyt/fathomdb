## 2. The 3 design improvements (the gaps that caused ~$15 of waste)
1. **AUTO-RESUME by default.** On startup the priced runner must auto-detect `<output>.checkpoint.json`
   (and/or an explicit `--resume`/preserved path) and **load it without a manual flag**, skipping
   already-answered cells. Any restart (window-eviction, manual, crash) then continues seamlessly with
   ZERO re-spend and NO restart-from-scratch. (The current `--resume` is opt-in â†’ relaunches forgot it â†’
   loops + re-spend. Make auto the default; `--resume <path>` is an explicit override / alternate source.)
2. **A failed call is NOT an abstention.** Today a retry-exhausted failure (429/5xx/timeout after backoff)
   degrades to `None` â‡’ scored F1=0 â‡’ deflates the arm AND is never retried. Fix: distinguish
   **abstention** (a SUCCESSFUL call where the reader returned no usable answer â€” a legitimate `None`, scored
   0, not retried) from **failure** (call never succeeded â€” marked MISSING, excluded from the checkpoint's
   answered set, re-called on the next resume, and counted against `answer_completeness`). Never persist a
   failed cell as a scored abstention.
3. **Atomic, frequent checkpoint.** Write the checkpoint via temp-file + `os.replace` (atomic) so a death
   mid-write can't corrupt it; checkpoint at least every ~10 questions (or every K cells). The
   `answer_completeness` guard already marks a run INVALID below the floor â€” keep it, now fed by the
   failure/abstention distinction.
