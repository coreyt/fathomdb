## Numbering ‚Äî N-mod-5 with reserved gaps (carried HITL directive)

Planned slices are numbered **only in multiples of 5**. The gaps
(`1‚Äì4, 6‚Äì9, 11‚Äì14, 16‚Äì19, 21‚Äì24, 26‚Äì29, 31‚Äì34, 36‚Äì39, 41‚Äì44`) are the **insertion
mechanism** for unplanned follow-on a slice reveals while executing.

**Reserved-gap policy (unchanged from 0.8.0).**

- Follow-on that must land before N+5 becomes Slice **N+1‚Ä¶N+4** ‚Äî a **fully
  orchestrated slice** (own prompt, own worktree from a fresh baseline, own
  `output.json`, own codex ¬ß9 review, own CLOSED block) ‚Äî NOT an ad-hoc patch.
- **Reserved-gap ‚âÝ fix-N.** fix-N re-enters the **same** slice's worktree to clear
  a BLOCK/CONCERN on that slice's own deliverable; a reserved-gap slice is **new
  adjacent scope** (fresh baseline + worktree).
- **HALT rule:** if a gap band (N+1‚Ä¶N+4) fills and follow-on remains, the slice was
  mis-scoped ‚Üí **HALT to HITL**. Never overflow into the next mod-5 number.

**Pre-identified reserved-gap candidates** (see per-slice "Reserved follow-on" in
`0.8.1-implementation.md`):

  band 31‚Äì34 (after R3) or 36‚Äì39.
