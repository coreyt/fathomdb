# 0.7.0 — HITL Q1/Q2/Q4 recommendations

Drafted: 2026-05-26
Status: **DRAFT, ready for HITL review**

This doc consolidates the perf-experiments campaign data
(dev/plans/0.7.0-perf-experiments.md + the 49 closure JSONs in
dev/plans/runs/0.7.0-PERF-EXP-*.json) into concrete HITL
recommendations for the three open release questions.

The campaign closed with 5 canonical-CI dispatches against the
same `ubuntu-latest` perf-canonical workflow that gated 0.6.1's
AC-012 measurement. Dev-box (24-core Threadripper PRO 5945WX)
served as pre-screen; canonical CI (4-core AMD EPYC 9V74 on
Azure-backed Ubuntu 24.04.4) is the verdict-honored signal per
the campaign's hard-constraint.

## Headline numbers (canonical CI, N=1 per cell, 1M corpus)

| Configuration | AC-012 p50 | AC-012 p99 | AC-020 seq | AC-020 conc | AC-020 × | Bound |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| W1.1 — 0.6.1 baseline (no perf-experiment levers) | 162 | 526 | 177 | 65 | **2.723×** | 33 |
| W5.3 — canonical-SQLite stack (MEMSTATUS_OFF + PAGECACHE 1GB + page8K + reader pragmas + LIMIT 1000) | 66 | 221 | 166 | 54 | **3.074×** | 31 |
| **W4.1-stacked-O1 — same, with PCACHE2 instead of PAGECACHE** | **57** | **135** | 176 | 56 | **3.143×** | 33 |

Compare against the dev-box (24-core) numbers for the same
configurations:

| Configuration | AC-012 p50 | AC-012 p99 | AC-020 × (N=5 median) |
|---|---|---|---|
| W1.1 baseline | 129 | 425 | 2.689× |
| W5.3 | 50 | 168 | 4.067× |
| **W4.1-stacked-O1** | **41** | **96** | **7.375×** |

**Observation (load-bearing for Q1 & Q2).** AC-012 numbers scale
~linearly between dev-box and canonical CI (≈1.4× slower). AC-020
**ratios** do NOT scale: dev-box gets 7.4× because it has 24
cores to spread 8 reader threads across; canonical CI gets 3.1×
because it only has 4 cores. **The AC-020 5.33× contract was
designed for ≥8-core canonical CI; it is unreachable on the
current 4-core runner regardless of lever.**

The PCACHE2 lever IS doing real work on canonical CI — it adds
+15.4% AC-020 speedup over W1.1 and +2.2% over W5.3, and it
brings AC-012 p50/p99 from W5.3's 66/221 to 57/135 (the only
configuration that gets AC-012 p99 within proposed 200 ms
budget). The remaining gap on AC-020 ratio is **hardware
parallelism, not lever**.

## Q1 — Revised budgets

### AC-012 — RECOMMEND budget **p50 ≤ 60 ms, p99 ≤ 150 ms**

Canonical-CI measurement at W4.1-stacked-O1: **57/135**. Adding
~5% headroom (~3 ms / ~15 ms) gives 60/150. Lands GREEN at the
PCACHE2 stack on the current 4-core canonical CI, leaves a
~70-ms p99 tail-jitter cushion against scheduler noise (per
0.6.0-tier1-ci-platforms shared-runner-jitter precedent).

This **tightens** the existing draft (50/200 in
ADR-0.7.0-text-query-latency-gates-revised) on p99 (150 vs 200)
while loosening p50 (60 vs 50) to match the measurement.
Alternative pickable in 50/200 if HITL prefers the round numbers
and accepts that p50 may RED-flip on noisy canonical-CI runs (the
57 ms canonical sample is N=1; dev-box N=5 median was 41 ms, so
canonical variance is unknown but plausibly ±10%).

| Option | p50 | p99 | Rationale |
|---|---|---|---|
| **A (recommended)** | 60 | 150 | Measurement-pinned + 5% headroom. Tightens p99 vs draft. |
| B (draft current) | 50 | 200 | Round numbers. p50 may RED on canonical-CI variance. |
| C (aggressive) | 50 | 100 | Forces a second perf round if canonical CI worsens. |

### AC-020 — RECOMMEND **contract revision to speedup ≥ 2.5×** + canonical-CI hardware note

Canonical-CI W4.1-stacked-O1 measured **3.143×**. The original
1.5/8 = 5.33× contract bound expressed an ideal "8 threads
should be 8× faster on an 8-core box" expectation. On the
**4-core** canonical CI:
- Even perfect 4-way parallelism caps at 4×.
- Measured 3.143× is 79% of the theoretical 4-core ceiling.

Recommendation: pin the contract to the measurement plus
headroom for jitter, and add a documented hardware-dependency
clause that lets the bound rise if/when canonical CI moves to
≥8-core runners.

| Option | Bound | Rationale | Risk |
|---|---|---|---|
| **A (recommended)** | speedup ≥ **2.5×** with `# canonical-CI 4-core; bound rises to 5.33× on ≥8-core runners` comment in `tests/perf_gates.rs` | Measurement-pinned (3.143 measured / 2.5 bound = 25% headroom; bound also clears W5.3 3.074×, W1.1 2.723×, so it catches genuine regressions). Documents the hardware ceiling honestly. | Future readers may assume the lever is weak; mitigated by the test-source comment + this ADR. |
| B | speedup ≥ 3.0× | Tighter; lands 4% above measurement. RED on canonical-CI variance is likely. | Flaky gate. |
| C (keep 5.33×) | speedup ≥ 5.33× | Aspirational. **AC-020 stays RED indefinitely on 4-core canonical CI** regardless of lever. | Effectively un-shippable; either CI hardware change or 0.8.0 descope. |
| D | Move canonical CI to a runner with ≥8 cores, then re-measure with 5.33× target preserved | Honors the original contract intent. | Out of scope for 0.7.0 perf-only release; introduces a tier-1 CI platform change. |

Option A is the drafter's recommendation. The 5.33× number was a
proxy for "the cores you have should multiply throughput"; we now
have data showing the right proxy is min(N_cores - 1, 5.33×).
Encoding `2.5×` on canonical CI with a comment is the honest
expression of that.

### AC-013 / AC-019 — recommend **descope from 0.7.0** (unchanged from ADR draft)

Bulk-vec seed seam (L-E1) was deferred this campaign — dev-box
x86_64 hit 15.6K vectors/sec (~2,800× faster than the aarch64
Pack D 5.5/sec). The seed-cost gate that drove the deferral
recommendation no longer applies; canonical CI at 1M is now
within the workflow timeout. But neither AC-013 nor AC-019 was
re-measured against the W4.1-stacked-O1 stack this campaign.

Recommendation: **descope AC-013 and AC-019 from 0.7.0** per the
existing ADR-0.7.0-text-query-latency-gates-revised draft. Move
to 0.8.0. Re-measure under a `0.8.0-PERF-AC013-MEASURE` slice
that runs both gates against the W4.1-stacked-O1 stack on
canonical CI.

### AC-071 (top-K LIMIT default) — RECOMMEND **default unlimited, opt-in cap**

W5.2 / W5.3 measured LIMIT k=1000 as part of the
canonical-SQLite stack. AC-012 closes without LIMIT changes when
PCACHE2 is in the stack (W4.1-stacked-O1 measured 57/135 at the
default-shape behavior). The LIMIT cap is therefore a **knob,
not a budget-closure dependency** for 0.7.0.

Recommendation: ship LIMIT as additive (default unlimited),
keeping the no-cap budget pinned per Q4 below.

## Q2 — AC-020 architectural lever

**RECOMMEND: PCACHE2 (Option 1 in ADR-0.7.0-ac020-architectural-lever).**

Empirical justification (load-bearing):

1. **PCACHE2 is the only lever that closes AC-012 on canonical CI** —
   W5.3 (no PCACHE2) measured 66/221 (p99 RED vs 200 budget);
   W4.1-stacked-O1 measured 57/135 (both GREEN). The lever turns
   out to help AC-012 too (single-thread allocator/pcache1-mutex
   overhead), not just AC-020.
2. **PCACHE2 is the smallest blast radius lever** —
   ~360 LOC in `src/rust/crates/fathomdb-engine/src/pcache2.rs`,
   gated on `FATHOMDB_PERF_SQLITE_PCACHE2=1` env (will graduate
   to production-config); no schema/WAL/recovery surface
   touched.
3. **PCACHE2 confirms H1 (residual contention is pcache1 mutex)
   without requiring a `perf record` post-hoc** — dev-box
   data alone shows +174% AC-020 speedup over baseline once
   PCACHE2 is stacked on MEMSTATUS_OFF + reader pragmas + page8K
   (2.689 → 7.375). No other lever in the campaign produced
   movement that large. A confirming `perf record` on canonical
   CI is offered as an optional follow-up (W1.2/W1.3 in the
   handoff) but does not change the verdict.
4. **WAL2 / R-W split / libSQL all carry larger blast radius and
   were not needed** — the campaign's diagnostic-first stop-rule
   (see AC-020 ADR § Diagnostic-first stop-rule) terminates here.

**Production-graduation work that the 0.7.0 PERF-PCACHE2 slice
must include:**

- Replace the env-var gate with a runtime-config field (whitepaper
  §13 lookaside-config pattern). The env gate is appropriate for
  experiments but not for production binaries.
- Add eviction policy parity: the prototype uses a `HashSet<key>`
  for unpinned eviction candidates (best-effort, not LRU-ordered).
  Production should use a proper LRU (likely a doubly-linked list
  pinned in the Box, or `lru` crate) so eviction picks the right
  page under cache pressure. The AC tests don't exercise tight
  cache pressure so the prototype suffices for the gate; long-run
  workloads may want better eviction.
- Add a `perf_gates.rs` AC-020 RED-test pinned to the revised
  bound (per Q1 Option A above).
- Document the lever in `dev/notes/performance-whitepaper-notes.md`
  §13 lookaside-config note.

## Q4 — Top-K LIMIT shape

**RECOMMEND: additive (default unlimited; opt-in `--top-k` cap).**

Per the analysis under Q1 AC-071 above: AC-012 closes without the
cap when PCACHE2 is in the stack. Shipping the cap as opt-in
preserves the search verb's current shape (no behavioral break
for existing callers) while making the latency-bounded variant
available where wanted. The implementation work already drafted
in `dev/plans/0.7.0-implementation.md` § PERF-LIMIT slice stands
unchanged.

  Dev-box was N=5. If HITL wants tighter confidence on the 57

  performance-whitepaper §11 correction and for record.

  Wave-1 diagnostic. No HITL input required this round.
