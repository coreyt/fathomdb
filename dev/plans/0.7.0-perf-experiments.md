# 0.7.0 perf-experiments — design + log

This doc plans and tracks the experiment campaign that produces
the data required to lock HITL Q1 (revised budgets), Q2 (AC-020
lever), and Q4 (top-K LIMIT shape).

Posture: **measure before deciding.** The 0.6.0 budgets were
locked on theory and the canonical-runner measurement returned
RED by 7×. We do not repeat that pattern: every lever in the
0.7.0 menu gets a measurement-backed verdict before any lock
proposal.

## Why an experiment campaign rather than a HITL lock first

The four planning-slice questions (Q1..Q4) all carry an
empirical risk-of-being-wrong:

- **Q1 (revised budgets)** depends on which levers actually
  move the canonical-runner numbers. A budget of "p50 ≤ 50 ms"
  is defensible only after we know what shapes the architecture
  can hit.
- **Q2 (AC-020 lever)** depends on which mutex / contention
  primitive actually dominates at canonical scale. Three
  hypotheses (H1 / H2 / H3 per
  `dev/notes/performance-whitepaper-notes.md § 6`) exist; one
  diagnostic pass disambiguates.
- **Q3 (diagnostic-first)** is the experiment-campaign meta-
  decision. This doc IS the diagnostic-first execution; Q3 is
  not a question, it is a method.
- **Q4 (LIMIT shape)** depends on whether the LIMIT cap actually
  closes the AC-012 gap. If LIMIT does not move the canonical
  number, the additive-cap proposition is empty; if LIMIT closes
  the gap unconditionally, breaking-shape is more attractive
  than additive-with-default-unlimited.

## Experiment matrix (waves)

Experiments are executed in waves. Each wave's results inform the
next wave's design. Wall-clock latency is the gate, not session
boundaries — a wave may span multiple sessions.

## Decision rules (universal)

For every source-change lever, KEEP/REVERT rule per
`feedback_reliability_principles` + `feedback_tdd`:

- **KEEP** iff the canonical CI measurement meets the lever's
  target AC budget (or makes monotone progress toward the
  revised budget pinned at HITL Q1 lock) AND `agent-verify` is
  GREEN AND codex review returns PASS.
- **REVERT** iff the canonical CI measurement does not improve
  the target AC by ≥ 5% on its load-bearing percentile.
- **INCONCLUSIVE** iff dev-box pre-screen says improvement but
  canonical CI says no improvement. Surface to HITL; do not
  silently land the change.

All numeric verdicts are captured in
`dev/plans/runs/0.7.0-PERF-EXP-<wave>-<id>-output.json` per
schema below.

**Wave 4 finding (corroborated, then refined).** Seven dev-box
PRAGMA-tier configurations (W2.1-W2.4 + W4.0a-W4.0c) all land
within ±5% of baseline speedup, even with the most aggressive
PRAGMA (synchronous=OFF + wal_autocheckpoint=0). **PRAGMA-tier
fully exhausted for AC-020** per whitepaper §12 prediction.

**Then W4.0d broke the canonical-SQLite barrier.**
`SQLITE_CONFIG_MEMSTATUS=0` (process-start FFI, whitepaper §7.4,
listed but never landed) drops the allocator-stats locking
surface. **Single-lever dev-box impact: AC-020 speedup 2.689× →
4.033× (+50%).** Stacked with reader PRAGMA + synchronous=NORMAL
(W4.0e): **speedup 4.393× (+63% vs baseline).**

This is the largest AC-020 movement achieved by any
canonical-SQLite lever in the Pack 5 / 6 / 6.G / 0.7.0
campaigns combined. It corrects whitepaper §11's conclusion
("the A.2 hot symbols are not SQLite threading-mode mutexes" —
