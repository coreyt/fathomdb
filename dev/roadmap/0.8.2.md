## 0. Why 0.8.2 changed shape

The 0.8.1 "beat BM25" investigation reached a robust NEGATIVE — but a **narrow** one
(`../plans/runs/0.8.1-beat-bm25-report.md`): it measured the **memory/fact-on-edge** structure on the
**graph-disfavored axis** (first-stage needle-recall) with **disfavored seeding** (the graph's own
FTS, not the lexical top-K). It does not speak to multi-hop **answer accuracy** or **sensemaking** —
the axes graphs are *claimed* to win — because we never measured those and lacked the instruments.
Three versions build the instruments and adjudicate fairly:

| Ver | Initiative | Axis tested | Plan |
|---|---|---|---|
| **0.8.2** | **M1** — lexically-seeded PPR arm on MuSiQue, EM/F1 by hop count | multi-hop **answer accuracy** | [`../plans/plan-0.8.2.md`](../plans/plan-0.8.2.md) |
| **0.8.3** | **M2** — +2Wiki +MultiHop-RAG, +IRCoT iterative arm, +R5 PRF | full multi-hop study | [`0.8.3.md`](0.8.3.md) |

## 1. M1 in one paragraph

Build a per-question graph over MuSiQue-Ans paragraphs with the existing **Qwen3.6-27B** extractor
($0 local), run a **HippoRAG-style lexically-seeded Personalized-PageRank arm fused with BM25**, and
measure **EM/F1 broken out by hop count (2/3/4)** against a **strong** baseline (BM25 ∪ passage-dense ∪
fused) using the **same answerer** across arms. PPR strictly dominates the 0.8.1 raw-BFS arm, so if
PPR-fusion can't beat the baseline, BFS never would — a clean adjudication. It is an **eval-harness
initiative, not an engine change** (PPR runs in-harness over the graph; CPU-only, no-API).

## 2. Honest expectation (carry it)

Research (2026-06-16) says the likely outcome is **near-tie to modest loss** vs a strong baseline —
graph multi-hop wins are single-digit F1 and shrink to ~0 against strong dense (GraphRAG-Bench
arXiv:2506.05690; RAG-vs-GraphRAG arXiv:2502.11371). M1 is still worth running: it converts *"we tried
a weak BFS arm"* into *"we tried the literature's best lightweight graph method and it still didn't
win"* — the result that earns the index-key-enrichment pivot. The *proven* multi-hop lever is
iterative retrieval (IRCoT), which lands in 0.8.3, not here.

## 3. Pre-registered decision rule (frozen at plan Slice 0)

- **GO → 0.8.3 (M2):** PPR-fusion shows a material, dose-responsive lift on ≥3-hop answerable EM/F1.
- **NO-GO → record the negative, redirect to index-key enrichment:** flat-or-negative ⇒ graph
  topology adds no multi-hop answer signal — the strongest fair version of the graph hypothesis,
  refuted. 0.8.3/0.8.4 may still proceed (different structures/axes) with a "prior: negative" framing.

## 4. Scope, slices, reuse

Full slice contracts (Slice 0 design+pre-register → 5 baseline bar → 10 graph build → 15 PPR-fusion
KEYSTONE → 20 verdict) and the reuse inventory are in [`../plans/plan-0.8.2.md`](../plans/plan-0.8.2.md).
M1 adds no new infra beyond a MuSiQue acquire+hash, a ~tens-of-lines PPR pass (scipy/networkx), and an
EM/F1 + hop-strata scorer; everything else (extractor, FTS5, passage-dense, answerer, harness patterns)
is reused from 0.8.1.
