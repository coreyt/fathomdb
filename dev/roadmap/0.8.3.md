# FathomDB 0.8.3 — index-key enrichment + dense retrieval (post-M1 redirect)

> **REDIRECTED 2026-06-19 by the M1 verdict (HITL-signed).** 0.8.3 was scoped as **M2 — the full
> multi-hop graph study** (+2Wiki +MultiHop-RAG +IRCoT). The 0.8.2 **M1 NO-GO** removed its premise:
> the multi-hop graph arm is now refuted on **both** axes —
> recall ([`../plans/runs/0.8.1-beat-bm25-report.md`](../plans/runs/0.8.1-beat-bm25-report.md), n=40) and
> answer-accuracy ([`../plans/runs/0.8.2-m1-FINDINGS.md`](../plans/runs/0.8.2-m1-FINDINGS.md), n=300).
> Per the registered M1 decision, **0.8.3 redirects away from graph traversal toward the two non-graph
> levers M1 pointed at.** The former M2 graph study is **dropped** (see §3).

## 0. What M1 told us to do next

The M1 5-arm adjudication on MuSiQue multi-hop QA (same answerer, n=300) ranked:
**`passage_dense` 0.487 > `fused-RRF` 0.450 > `fused_rerank` 0.415 > `ppr_fusion` (graph) 0.410 >
`bm25` 0.370.** Two findings drive 0.8.3:
1. **Graph structure was not where the multi-hop signal lived — dense passage retrieval was.**
   `passage_dense` beat every fused/graph arm. That is an *observation, not a registered comparison*
   (M1's registered endpoint was graph-vs-fused), so 0.8.3 promotes it to a **registered** question.
2. **Index-key enrichment remains the one lexical lever with placebo-confirmed content value.** 0.8.1
   R6 measured (n=40): appending a doc's own extracted facts to its FTS content carried **+0.075 over a
   length-matched placebo** (genuine lexical bridge) though it didn't beat the FTS baseline *as-is*
   because of an FTS length-norm penalty. It was shelved with a clear "surface that content value"
   follow-up — which 0.8.3 now runs on the M1 multi-hop harness.

## 1. Scope (two registered directions, reusing the M1 harness)

The M1 harness (MuSiQue corpus + identical-answerer EM/F1 + per-hop strata + paired-bootstrap
`decide()` + resilient priced runner) is reused wholesale; both directions are **non-graph retrieval**
and stay within the CPU/no-API/1-bit footprint.

- **D1 — passage-dense, registered.** Promote `passage_dense` from M1 observation to a pre-registered
  comparison vs fused-RRF on multi-hop answer accuracy; test dense-strengthening variants (e.g. the
  **engine CLS-vs-Mean pooling bug** flagged in M1 — `CandleBgeEmbedder` defaults to `Mean` for a
  CLS-pooled model, degrading shipped dense retrieval; STATUS-0.8.2 tracked-bug) and **R5 — vector
  pseudo-relevance feedback** (the cheap, no-LLM query-side lever folded in from IR-C; its natural home
  is dense retrieval, not the now-dropped IRCoT arm).
  - **D1 internal sequence (ADJ-5, 2026-06-19 — from the independent confirmation pass).** Run D1 in
    two sub-steps, not one: **(D1a)** land the CLS-pooling fix, re-clear eu7 ≥0.90, and **re-measure the
    corrected dense/fused baseline first** — adding no new retrieval machinery; **(D1b)** only then add
    **deterministic vector PRF** as a treatment vs that corrected baseline. Rationale: the two
    independent codex passes agreed on every approach and the dense-first ordering, but diverged on
    where PRF sits — the steered pass made PRF a core first move, the independent pass deferred it until
    the corrected-dense baseline exists. Establishing the clean baseline before PRF prevents attributing
    a pooling-fix gain to PRF (and vice-versa) and keeps each lever individually falsifiable.
- **D2 — index-key enrichment, registered + scaled.** Revive 0.8.1 R6 (`eval/r6_index_key_enrichment.py`,
  7 ACs, placebo control) and measure enriched-vs-plain on the **M1 multi-hop** harness with the
  length-matched placebo, at adequate N. Pair with the **length-norm fix** R6 implicated (the lexical
  content value was real but eaten by FTS length-normalization) — see the deferred tunable-/lower-`b`
  FTS5 lever in [`0.8.5.md`](0.8.5.md), now potentially **promoted** here if D2's content value
  reproduces at scale.

## 1a. Adjustments (2026-06-19, from the codex confirmation pass)

Four adjustments folded in after the codex forward-hypothesis review
([`../plans/runs/fathomdb-next-steps-codex-hypothesis.md`](../plans/runs/fathomdb-next-steps-codex-hypothesis.md)):

- **ADJ-1 — Re-pre-register the endpoint for feasible power; lead GO on the LLM-free metrics.** The M1
  `decide()` rule was **underpowered even at N=1165** (P(GO)≈0.45 at materiality 0.04), and the MuSiQue
  ΔF1 endpoint is the *priced* one. So for D1/D2 the **primary GO signal is the cheap, LLM-free
  endpoints** — MuSiQue **all-bridges@K** (bridge presence is the measured F1 driver, 0.510 vs 0.068),
  **eu8 relevance recall@10** + the ~596-query hard subset, and **LongMemEval R@10/@20 per-class** —
  with the **priced MuSiQue pooled ≥3-hop answer-F1 as confirmatory**. Before any priced pass,
  re-pre-register an **MDE-feasible materiality (or larger N)** so a MuSiQue result is decisive either
  way; keep the registered comparator = fused-RRF (k=60).
- **ADJ-2 — D2 pulls F5 + tunable-`b` engine work forward from 0.8.5 (scope decision).** Codex's D2 is a
  **fielded / sidecar enrichment channel (NOT blind body-append)** + a length-norm fix — which is
  exactly the **F5 (fielded-FTS / BM25F)** and **tunable-/lower-`b` FTS5** levers currently parked in
  [`0.8.5.md`](0.8.5.md). Adopting D2 as the strong form therefore **promotes both 0.8.5 engine levers
  into 0.8.3** (re-point the F5 ADR accordingly). The weak fallback is body-append+placebo only (the
  0.8.1 R6 form), which already underperformed FTS as-is — so the fielded form is the version worth
  running. **Decide at the D2 design gate:** promote F5+tunable-`b`, or defer D2.
- **ADJ-3 — D1 must re-clear the eu7 0.90 fidelity floor.** The CLS-pooling fix **changes the stored
  vectors**, so the 1-bit binary index must **re-pass eu7 ≥0.90** (the large-run value was **0.896**,
  below floor) before any product promotion — measure, do not assume the fix is fidelity-neutral.
- **ADJ-4 — D1 target = bridge presence AND composition, not raw dense recall.** Dense retrieves bridges
  *more often* (all-bridges@10 0.68 vs fused 0.65) but **composes answers worse** (F1-given-all-bridges
  0.464 vs fused 0.552). So D1 must lift bridge presence **without** degrading fused's composition; the
  registered comparator stays **fused-RRF**, **all-bridges@K is a registered metric**, and **CE-rerank
  stays a logged secondary** (it was null on M1), not a default arm.

## 1b. Capability-report adjustments (2026-06-21, ADJ-6..10 — measurement scope HITL-approved)

Five adjustments folded in after the 2026-06-21 capability-status-report review
([`../plans/runs/0.8.x-capability-status-report.md`](../plans/runs/0.8.x-capability-status-report.md)).
The report's two load-bearing facts: (i) the dense arm is **embedder-bound** (eu8 relevance ceiling
≈0.571 on `bge-small-en-v1.5`; competitors beat BM25 on BEIR by 15–25% via stronger embedders), and
(ii) FathomDB has **no end-to-end agentic-memory accuracy number** vs Mem0/Zep — the product's defining
axis is unmeasured (answerer + Mem0-OSS blocked; four memory classes at N=0 gold since 0.8.1 Slice 25).
**The measurement + prerequisite work below is HITL-approved (2026-06-21);** engine *promotions* still
route through the Slice-0 gate.

- **ADJ-6 — Embedder-ceiling probe BEFORE the D1b PRF build (HITL-approved, $0, LLM-free).** D1a fixes
  pooling and D1b adds vector PRF, but **neither raises the model or its ceiling** — PRF only retrieves
  more neighbours from the same embedder. So before committing the in-engine PRF build, run a $0 A/B of
  the **corrected `bge-small` ceiling vs 1–2 stronger CPU/ONNX-runnable embedders** (`bge-base`,
  `gte-small`, `e5-small`) on the frozen eu8 set + the ~596-q hard subset, within the candle/ONNX
  footprint (`EmbedderChoice::Caller`+ONNX, see [[embed-completeness-and-gpu-readiness]]). **Gate:** D1b
  PRF proceeds only if the corrected-dense arm shows headroom under the best feasible embedder; if a
  stronger embedder breaks the ceiling, **that** becomes the D1 lever and PRF is reconsidered.
- **ADJ-7 — D2 content-value harness-proxy at scale BEFORE the fielded-FTS engine migration
  (HITL-approved, $0, LLM-free).** D2's last data point (0.8.1 R6, n=40) **lost to FTS-only as-is** and
  only beat a placebo; committing a schema migration + BM25F on a lever behind the baseline is premature.
  Insert a $0 harness-only proxy: measure fielded-enrichment content value at scale in an **in-harness
  separate index (no migration)** — confirm (a) content beats the length-matched placebo at adequate N
  and (b) fielding removes the FTS length-norm penalty R6 implicated. The engine migration proceeds only
  on a passing proxy. This **extends cheap-validate-before-spend to cheap-validate-before-engine**.
- **ADJ-8 — D0: agentic-memory measurement-unblock as a first-class, HITL-approved parallel track.** The
  retrieval levers do not make the product's parity claim falsifiable. **D0 runs parallel and
  non-blocking:** (D0a) re-pin/rebuild the gold corpus so `temporal`/`multi_hop`/`knowledge_update`/
  `multi_session` carry **non-zero gold**, and wire the answerer seam (`R2_RUN=1`); (D0b) stand up a
  working **local Mem0-OSS baseline**. Goal: make the end-to-end parity number **producible within
  0.8.3** even if the priced number lands later. The R2 metric/threshold stays a HITL eval gate, but the
  unblock + prerequisite corpus/baseline work is approved. **Scope:** D0 is **Mem0-only** (Mem0-type
  function); the **Graphiti/Zep** (agentic-memory) and **GraphRAG/HippoRAG** (multi-hop) as-is
  head-to-heads are deferred to the **0.8.4 known-gap** ([`0.8.4.md`](0.8.4.md) §5).
- **ADJ-9 — Latency-regression is a DoD gate on every engine / read-path slice (HITL-approved).** Engine
  performance (the "high-performing embedded database" claim) has **PENDING** budgets, and 0.8.3 adds
  read-path cost (PRF re-query, BM25F columns, custom-`b` ranking). **Re-measure AC012/AC013/AC020** in
  the DoD of the engine slices and the fused-stack verdict; a budget breach → **HITL**, same pattern as
  the eu7 fidelity gate. Do not ship retrieval quality at an unmeasured latency cost.
- **ADJ-10 — D1 registered question = corrected-FUSED vs corrected-DENSE, composition a CO-PRIMARY.**
  Dense's specific weakness is **composition** (F1-given-all-bridges 0.464 vs fused 0.552), which PRF
  cannot fix. Reframe D1 from "promote dense" to "**does a corrected/strengthened dense MEMBER improve
  the fused stack**" — fused-RRF stays both comparator and likely keeper. Promote **all-bridges@K
  (presence)** and **F1-given-all-bridges (composition)** to co-primary LLM-free endpoints; a dense arm
  that wins presence but loses composition is a **NO-GO**.
- **Both-null fallback (pre-registered at Slice 0).** If D1 and D2 are both flat at the strong-baseline
  floor, 0.8.3 does **not** redirect open-endedly; the pre-registered next fork is (a) **embedder
  upgrade** (the ADJ-6 outcome), (b) the **reader / answer-synthesis axis** (the report's ~5× reader
  swing — possibly the real product lever), or (c) finish **D0** so parity can at least be claimed/bounded.

## 2. Method notes (carried from M1)

- **Same fairness discipline:** strong baseline (BM25 ∪ passage-dense ∪ fused-RRF), same answerer/depth
  across arms, pre-registered primary endpoint + power/MDE + frozen `decide()`, versioned artifacts, $
  ledger + cheap-validate before priced runs, resilient-by-construction priced harness
  ([[priced-runs-need-resilience-before-spend]]).
- **Placebo discipline for D2:** length-matched foreign-token placebo separates lexical-bridge content
  value from the length artifact (the R6 design, codex-reviewed).
- **Honesty prior:** the 0.8.1+0.8.2 record is that *no cheap retrieval lever has yet beaten strong
  lexical/dense*. D1/D2 are the two with a live, evidence-backed rationale; both carry a real chance of
  another null. Pre-register so a null is a clean, publishable negative, not a moved goalpost.

## 3. Dropped / deferred by the M1 redirect

- **M2 graph-multi-hop study (2WikiMultiHopQA path-match, MultiHop-RAG, PPR on more datasets) —
  DROPPED.** Graph multi-hop is refuted twice (recall + answer-accuracy); spending more on additional
  multi-hop *graph* datasets is low-value. The 2Wiki gold-reasoning-path diagnostic is archived as a
  *possible* future probe only if a fundamentally different graph mechanism is proposed.
- **IRCoT iterative-retrieval arm — deferred, de-scoped from 0.8.3.** IRCoT is a *non-graph* multi-hop
  lever (iterative retrieve↔reason) and remains the literature's proven multi-hop driver, but it raises
  per-query LLM cost and is orthogonal to the retrieval-quality focus of D1/D2. Re-propose as its own
  initiative if multi-hop answer accuracy becomes a product target. (It was never graph-dependent, so
  the M1 NO-GO does not refute it — it is simply out of the redirected 0.8.3 scope.)
- **GraphRAG sensemaking (S1)** stays at **0.8.4** — a different structure on a different axis,
  untouched by the multi-hop verdicts (see [`0.8.4.md`](0.8.4.md)).
